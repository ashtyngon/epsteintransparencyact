Mortimer Benjamin Zuckerman, born June 4, 1937, in Montreal, is a Canadian-American billionaire. According to his Wikipedia article, he is the owner and editor-in-chief of *U.S. News & World Report*, and he owned the *New York Daily News* from 1993 until 2017. He is also co-founder and executive chairman of the real estate investment trust Boston Properties, and previously owned *The Atlantic* and *Fast Company*.

Zuckerman is named in reporting on the Jeffrey Epstein files in several documented contexts. His Wikipedia article states that he "contributed to a birthday book of letters for Jeffrey Epstein's 50th birthday," and that he "was identified as one of Jeffrey Epstein's clients, paying Epstein millions of dollars for estate planning and financial services between 2013 and 2015." Al Jazeera, reporting on the released files, similarly describes a financial relationship in which Zuckerman in 2008 sought Epstein's advice on passing on his estate — sharing sensitive financial details, including a copy of his will and an asset evaluation putting his net worth at $1.9 billion — and in which Epstein in 2013 proposed estate-planning services for a $30 million fee in June before offering a revised proposal of $21 million that December.

Al Jazeera also reports that Epstein sought to influence coverage in the *New York Daily News* while Zuckerman owned it. According to the outlet, an October 9, 2009 email shows Epstein sharing with Zuckerman a proposed response to questions from a *Daily News* journalist; Al Jazeera writes that the tabloid "first delayed its coverage of the allegations and then omitted details that the late financier had specifically requested be left out," and quotes an Epstein message asking to "take ghislaine out. if possible," referring to Ghislaine Maxwell. Al Jazeera further cites an August 24, 2014 email in which Zuckerman called Epstein "a great friend." The same article states that Zuckerman "has never been accused of any involvement in Epstein's crimes."
