John C. Phelan is a businessman and investor who served as the 79th United States Secretary of the Navy. According to his Wikipedia biography, he was nominated by President Trump in November 2024, confirmed by the Senate in March 2025, and served from March 25, 2025, to April 22, 2026. Earlier in his career he worked in finance, including as a co-founding partner and chief investment officer of MSD Capital, the private investment firm that managed Michael Dell's capital, before founding Rugger Management LLC in 2022.

According to Wikipedia, passenger manifests released as part of the Epstein files indicate that Phelan flew twice on Jeffrey Epstein's plane in 2006, several months before Epstein's first arrest on sex charges — a flight from New York to London on February 27, 2006, and a return flight from London to New York on March 3, 2006. CNN, in reporting republished by ABC17NEWS, and Navy Times both reported that the March 3, 2006 London-to-New York flight manifest was part of a document release from the House Oversight Committee.

Phelan declined to comment through a Navy spokesperson, according to the CNN report republished by ABC17NEWS and Navy Times. A close friend told CNN that this was the only time Phelan interacted with Epstein, that he had been invited to fly by Bear Stearns CEO Jimmy Cayne (who died in 2021), and that Phelan did not know they would be riding on Epstein's plane until they arrived. According to that account, Epstein pitched a tax concept during the flight that Phelan "had no interest in," and the two never interacted again. CNN reported there is no evidence Phelan knew of any wrongdoing by Epstein or his associates when he took the flight.
