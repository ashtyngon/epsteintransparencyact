Ehud Barak served as Prime Minister of Israel from July 1999 to March 2001, representing the Labor Party. His earlier career included the highest military rank in the Israel Defense Forces as Chief of General Staff, and he later served as Minister of Defense from 2007 to 2013.

Barak's association with Jeffrey Epstein is documented across released files and reporting. According to the Wikipedia compilation of people named in the Epstein files, the two regularly exchanged emails and met more than 60 times between September 2010 and March 2019, including during the period when Barak was Minister of Defense. Al Jazeera, drawing on released emails, reported that the relationship spanned roughly 15 years — Barak has said the two were introduced by former President Shimon Peres at a Washington event in 2003 — and involved business discussions around multiple Israeli technology ventures, including the startup later known as Carbyne. The reporting also describes Barak staying at Epstein's Manhattan home on multiple occasions between 2015 and 2019 and making one daytime visit to Epstein's Little Saint James island in the US Virgin Islands, which Barak said he attended accompanied by his wife and security guards.

In response to the renewed scrutiny, Barak has expressed regret while denying knowledge of Epstein's crimes. As reported by Al Jazeera, he stated that he regretted meeting Epstein and that "there is room to question whether I should have investigated more thoroughly. I regret not doing so." He said that across their association he "never saw any unreasonable occurrence" and that he "did not know the manner of his crimes until 2019." Through his lawyers he had earlier denied any awareness that Epstein "was paying underage girls for sex," and he says he cut ties after Epstein's 2019 arrest. Barak has not been accused of wrongdoing in connection with Epstein's offenses.
