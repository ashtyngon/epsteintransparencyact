Nathan Myhrvold is a former Microsoft chief technology officer who, according to his Wikipedia biography, became Microsoft's first CTO in 1996, founded Microsoft Research in 1991, and later co-founded the patent firm Intellectual Ventures in 2000 and the nuclear-energy company TerraPower. The same biography describes him as a working scientist, a prize-winning wildlife photographer, and the principal author of the *Modernist Cuisine* cookbook series.

Wikipedia's list of people named in the Epstein files states that "Epstein and Myhrvold frequently emailed each other and regularly met, both in Seattle and New York City, from at least 2010 to 2018," that Myhrvold invited Epstein to visit Intellectual Ventures in 2014, that he visited Epstein's U.S. Virgin Islands compound in 2011, and that he contributed wildlife photographs to Epstein's 2003 birthday book. KUOW reported that emails between the two contained "sexually explicit" joking language, including one exchange in which Myhrvold suggested Epstein "bring her" to visit an Intellectual Ventures lab and Epstein replied, "I'm going to leave her there," and that Myhrvold's name appeared in Epstein's 2003 birthday book. The Spokesman-Review reported that Myhrvold was listed as a passenger on Epstein's private plane on two 1990s flights (December 9, 1996, and January 11, 1997), was named as a contributor to the 2003 birthday book with a letter containing graphic animal imagery, and was listed in Epstein's address book.

In response to the reporting, Myhrvold said through a spokesperson, as quoted by The Spokesman-Review: "Mr. Myhrvold knew him from TED conferences and as a donor to basic scientific research. He regrets that he ever met him." His Wikipedia biography states that he "denied involvement in Epstein's criminal activities." KUOW reported that it had not heard of any Epstein victims accusing Myhrvold of unlawful behavior, nor seen evidence in the emails suggesting crimes.
