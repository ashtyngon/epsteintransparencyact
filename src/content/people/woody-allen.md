Woody Allen, born Allan Stewart Konigsberg, is an American filmmaker, writer, director, actor and comedian whose career spans more than six decades. He is known for films including *Annie Hall* (1977), which won four Academy Awards, *Manhattan* (1979), *Hannah and Her Sisters* (1986) and *Midnight in Paris* (2011), and has directed roughly 50 feature films over his career.

Allen is named in materials associated with the Jeffrey Epstein files. According to the Wikipedia summary of people named in the files, Allen was a guest at Epstein's Manhattan townhouse from 2010 onward and wrote a letter included in a book marking Epstein's 63rd birthday; photographs reportedly showed the two men together, including aboard a private plane. According to reporting by Variety and The Irish Times on emails released by the U.S. Justice Department, Allen and his wife Soon-Yi Previn often visited Epstein at his Upper East Side townhouse, and Epstein helped connect the family with Bard College president Leon Botstein in connection with their daughter Bechet's application. A January 11, 2017 message sent from Allen's account, which the reporting attributes to Previn, thanked Epstein "for getting Bechet into Bard." A spokesperson for Botstein said the applicant "was accepted on the merits of her own qualifications for admission" and characterized Epstein as "a serial liar." A representative for Allen did not respond to requests for comment for those reports.

In a September 13, 2025 interview with The Times, Allen described attending dinners at Epstein's home, saying there were "about 20 people there" whom he and Previn knew from show business and that he "didn't know Jeffrey at all then." Allen said Epstein "couldn't have been nicer," and recounted that Epstein told them he had "been in jail" but claimed he had been "extorted" and was "trying to make up for it now by being philanthropic." The Variety and Irish Times reports state that Allen has not been accused of any involvement in Epstein's alleged abuse.
