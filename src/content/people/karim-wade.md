Karim Wade is a Senegalese politician and the son of former president Abdoulaye Wade, who led Senegal from 2000 to 2012. According to his Wikipedia biography, he served as Minister of State for International Cooperation, Regional Development, Air Transport and Infrastructure from May 2009 to April 2012, a wide-ranging portfolio that led observers to describe him as a "super-minister." He was arrested in 2013 and, in March 2015, convicted of corruption and sentenced to six years in prison before being pardoned in June 2016, after which he went into exile in Qatar.

According to an investigation by the Organized Crime and Corruption Reporting Project (OCCRP), drawing on emails released by the US Justice Department, Jeffrey Epstein and Wade developed a relationship beginning in late 2010. OCCRP reports that in November 2010 Epstein wrote, "The President of Senegal is sending his son to see me in paris," and that the two later discussed business and investment ideas in Africa. An Agence France-Presse report published by Arab News states that Epstein viewed Wade as "one of the most important players in africa."

OCCRP and AFP report that after Wade's corruption conviction, Epstein helped finance lobbying efforts in the United States aimed at securing his release. According to OCCRP, a representative for Wade wrote to Epstein in September 2015 that the "Total cost for 3 months would be 100,000 USD," and an invoice from Wade's lawyer billed Epstein's Southern Trust Company $500,000 for legal-related fees. AFP reports that Epstein covered at least $50,000 in fees for the US lobbying firm Nelson Mullins, whose partner Robert Crowe kept Epstein updated on efforts to pressure Senegalese president Macky Sall. OCCRP states that it sent questions to the email address Wade had used in his correspondence with Epstein but did not receive a response, and AFP reports that Wade's lawyer, Mohamed Seydou Diagne, "did not consider it useful to comment" when contacted.
