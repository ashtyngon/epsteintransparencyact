Jack Horner is an American paleontologist. According to his Wikipedia biography, he discovered *Maiasaura* with Bob Makela in 1978 — work that provided the first evidence that some non-avian dinosaurs cared for their young — and he spent much of his career at the Museum of the Rockies and Montana State University before teaching at Chapman University. Wikipedia also notes that he served as the technical advisor for the first five *Jurassic Park* films and was "a partial inspiration for one of the lead characters of the franchise, Dr. Alan Grant."

Horner's documented appearance in connection with [Jeffrey Epstein](/people/jeffrey-epstein) stems from emails released as part of the Epstein files. According to the Daily Montanan, Horner visited Epstein's New Mexico ranch in the summer of 2012 for a paleontological tour, with planning emails spanning April through August 2012 and Horner arriving on August 3. The Daily Montanan reports that, afterward, Horner thanked Epstein's assistant and praised the hospitality, writing that although "we didn't find any dinosaur fossils," they noted "potential for marine reptiles." The same report says Epstein expressed interest in supporting Horner's "Dinochicken Project," an effort to reverse-engineer embryos to produce chickens resembling extinct species. The Daily Montanan states that Horner "could not be reached for comment" and that the article does not describe him as a victim, accuser, or wrongdoer — only as named in released correspondence.

According to The Panther, Chapman University's student newspaper, Chapman ended its employment relationship with Horner after the U.S. Department of Justice released emails linking him to Epstein, with his classes canceled in the days after the emails became public. The Panther reports that Chapman's director of strategic communications, Robert Hitchcock, confirmed Horner was no longer employed but did not say whether he was fired or left voluntarily, and did not confirm that the separation was due to the Epstein connection. Neither the Daily Montanan nor The Panther published a direct response from Horner or his representatives.
