## Is Jamie Dimon in the Epstein files?

Yes. Jamie Dimon (Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of JPMorgan Chase; longest-serving CEO of a major U.S. bank) is named in connection with the Jeffrey Epstein files. DOJ-released Epstein files, reported by Forbes and Bloomberg Law in July 2026, document that Dimon's name appears in two February 2010 scheduling emails released under the Epstein Files Transparency Act. In the first email, Epstein's longtime assistant Lesley Groff asked Epstein whether to prepare "heavy snacks" for "your evening appointments with Peter Mandelson, Jes Staley and Jamie Dimon" (the name Peter Mandelson had been redacted in the initial file release and was confirmed separately). In a second email sent in the early hours of 26 February 2010, Epstein himself wrote to Groff scheduling the same evening: "i have schedule [sic] ben at 230, tues, peter at 730 jes staley and dimon and [sic] 9," indicating a planned late-evening meeting at Epstein's Manhattan residence that would also include former UK Cabinet minister Peter Mandelson and then-JPMorgan executive Jes Staley. On 10 July 2026, Senator Elizabeth Warren sent Dimon a formal letter of inquiry as ranking member of the Senate Banking Committee, also citing emails between Mandelson and Epstein in which they reportedly discussed the possibility of Dimon calling then-UK Chancellor Alistair Darling about a proposed tax on bankers' bonuses — a call Dimon reportedly made. JPMorgan Chase stated in July 2026 that Dimon "never met with [Epstein], never emailed him, and was not involved in any decisions about his account," and that the bank "found no evidence that he was even invited to attend" the 2010 meeting; Dimon said in a 2023 deposition that he had "never had an appointment with Jeff Epstein." No wrongdoing is alleged against him.
