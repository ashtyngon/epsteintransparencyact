Marc Rowan is an American businessman who serves as chairman and chief executive officer of Apollo Global Management, a private equity and asset management firm he co-founded in 1990 alongside Josh Harris and Leon Black, according to his [Wikipedia biography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marc_Rowan). That article states he has served as CEO since March 2021 and that Apollo's assets under management crossed $1 trillion for the first time in the first quarter of 2026. It should not be confused with co-founder [Leon Black](/people/leon-black), a separate person whose paid relationship with [Jeffrey Epstein](/people/jeffrey-epstein) is documented elsewhere on this site.

Rowan's documented appearance in the Epstein material relates to firm tax matters. His Wikipedia biography states that "among the new documents released was a 2016 email exchange in which Rowan and Epstein discussed a potential Apollo corporate inversion, and a separate instance in which Rowan forwarded internal Apollo correspondence to Epstein relating to a tax matter." [InvestmentNews](https://www.investmentnews.com/regulation-legal-compliance/investors-sue-apollo-ceo-rowan-over-alleged-epstein-cover-up/266399), reporting in May 2026, similarly states that "Rowan forwarded a detailed internal calculation of Apollo's tax receivable agreement to Epstein in March 2016" and that "Epstein was involved in 2016 talks about a possible tax inversion that would have redomiciled Apollo overseas." His Wikipedia biography also reports that an earlier independent review by the law firm Dechert "found no evidence that Rowan or co-founder Josh Harris had hired Epstein or consulted with him on personal matters, and concluded that no Apollo employee other than Black had ever seriously considered retaining him."

Rowan and Apollo have disputed the characterization of any relationship. According to his Wikipedia biography, "Rowan and Apollo responded with a letter to clients stating that neither Rowan nor anyone else at the firm other than Black had a personal or business relationship with Epstein, while acknowledging that in limited instances Rowan and other employees had provided information to Epstein in connection with his personal tax advisory work for Black." [In Touch Weekly](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/shareholders-sue-apollo-global-management-180449766.html), in a report republished by Yahoo Finance dated March 3, 2026, quotes Rowan as saying of Epstein in a Bloomberg interview, "I didn't like him for my own reasons, he wasted my time. Even from the grave, he's wasting my time," and quotes an Apollo statement that "Neither Marc Rowan nor anyone else at Apollo (excluding Leon Black) had either a business or personal relationship with Jeffrey Epstein." Both InvestmentNews and In Touch Weekly report that shareholders have sued Apollo, Rowan and Black in federal court in Manhattan, alleging the firm misrepresented its relationship with Epstein in regulatory filings; InvestmentNews identifies one such case as Perez v. Apollo Global Management, filed April 29, 2026.

## Is Marc Rowan in the Epstein files?

Yes, in a documented but limited sense. Released Epstein documents include 2016 correspondence in which Rowan and Epstein discussed a potential Apollo corporate inversion and in which Rowan forwarded internal Apollo tax correspondence to Epstein, as reported by his Wikipedia biography and by InvestmentNews. Wikipedia's "List of people named in the Epstein files" does not give Rowan a standalone entry; it mentions him only in passing within Leon Black's entry. Rowan and Apollo say that neither he nor anyone at the firm other than Black had a business or personal relationship with Epstein, and that any contact concerned Epstein's tax work for Black. Rowan has not been charged with any crime, and being named in these documents is not evidence of any crime or wrongdoing.
