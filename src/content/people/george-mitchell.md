George J. Mitchell served as a United States Senator from Maine from 1980 to 1995, including six years as Senate Majority Leader (1989–1995). After leaving the Senate, he became one of America's most prominent diplomatic figures, serving as the U.S. Special Envoy for Northern Ireland and playing a central role in brokering the Good Friday Agreement in 1998, which ended decades of sectarian violence. He later served as U.S. Special Envoy for Middle East Peace from 2009 to 2011.

## How Mitchell Appears in the Records

Mitchell is not identified in flight logs. According to reporting on the released materials, his name appears in records tied to the Epstein investigations — including court documents containing an allegation by Virginia Giuffre, references in Epstein's so-called "birthday book," and email correspondence in which Epstein's staff sought to arrange meetings with him. The Bangor Daily News reported that Mitchell's name appears across the released files, and that messages showed Epstein attempting to contact him on multiple occasions after Epstein's 2008 conviction, including a 2010 message about a Yom Kippur event, a February 2013 reference to a possible lunch with Epstein and Bill Gates, and a November 2013 calendar entry listing an appointment. Mitchell's spokesman said that during the period between Epstein's conviction and his death, "members of Epstein's staff extended a small number of invitations to the Senator, all of which he declined or deflected."

In a statement, Mitchell said he "profoundly regrets ever having known Jeffrey Epstein and condemns, without reservation, the horrific harm Epstein inflicted on so many women."

## Allegation by Virginia Giuffre and Mitchell's Denial

Virginia Giuffre, an Epstein accuser who was publicly named in litigation before her death, alleged that Epstein directed or forced her to have sex with Mitchell when she was a minor in the early 2000s. The allegation was reported as having become public around 2020 and as appearing in records connected to the case. Mitchell has never been charged with any crime in connection with Epstein, and the allegation has not been tested at trial.

Mitchell has categorically denied the allegation. Through a spokesperson, he "reiterates unequivocally that he never met, spoke with, or had any contact of any kind with Ms Giuffre or with any underage women," and his representatives have attributed the claim to "a case of mistaken identity." His spokesman said that a 2021 photograph Giuffre supplied to a magazine had been incorrectly captioned as showing Mitchell standing behind Epstein, and that the individual pictured was not Mitchell.

## Resignation from Mitchell Institute

In early February 2026, Mitchell resigned as honorary chair of the Mitchell Institute, a Maine-based nonprofit that provides college scholarships to students from the state. After accepting his resignation, the institute's board said it was "an appropriate time to initiate a thoughtful, responsible process to consider a potential name change" — a step that would remove Mitchell's name from an organization that had been one of the most visible tributes to his public service.

## International Fallout

The institutional response extended across the Atlantic. Queen's University Belfast in Northern Ireland removed Mitchell's name from the Institute for Global Peace, Security and Justice and removed a bronze bust of him from campus. The US-Ireland Alliance, a nonprofit that administers a prestigious academic exchange scholarship named in his honor, said it would remove his name from the George J. Mitchell Scholarship Program. The breadth of the response — spanning Maine, Northern Ireland, and the U.S.–Ireland community — reflected both the scope of Mitchell's legacy and the gravity of the disclosures, which he disputes.
