Edward S. Boyden is an American neuroscientist at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where he holds the Y. Eva Tan Professorship in Neurotechnology and is a member of the McGovern Institute for Brain Research. He is best known for helping to pioneer optogenetics — a technique that uses light-activated proteins to control neurons — and for developing expansion microscopy, a method that physically enlarges biological samples so they can be imaged at high resolution with conventional microscopes.

Boyden's name appears in the Epstein files in connection with a single social event. According to the released files and reporting by The Daily Beast, Reid Hoffman hosted a 2015 dinner in Palo Alto in Boyden's honor. In an August 20, 2015 email to Tom Pritzker, [Jeffrey Epstein](/people/jeffrey-epstein) referenced the gathering, writing: "not sure yet. i had dinner with zuckerburg, mu=k, thiel hoffman, wild." The Daily Beast reported that Epstein also emailed himself a photo from the dinner showing Elon Musk, Mark Zuckerberg, Zuckerberg's wife Priscilla Chan, and others around a table. The Daily Beast described the gathering as a dinner "honoring scientists," and a spokesperson for Zuckerberg said he "met Epstein in passing one time at a dinner honoring scientists that was not organized by Epstein."

In the reporting reviewed for this profile, Boyden is named only as the honoree of the dinner; he is not described as having corresponded with Epstein, organized the event, or had any further contact with him. The Daily Beast articles quoted responses from other attendees — Musk said he did not recall introducing Epstein to anyone and called him "obviously a creep," while Hoffman said he "deeply" regretted interacting with Epstein after his conviction — but did not report any statement from Boyden himself. No response or comment from Edward Boyden was found in the sources reviewed for this profile.
