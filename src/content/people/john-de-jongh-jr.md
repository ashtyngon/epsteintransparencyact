John de Jongh Jr. is an American businessman and politician who served as the 7th Governor of the U.S. Virgin Islands from January 1, 2007 to January 5, 2015, according to his Wikipedia biography. Before entering elected office he worked in banking and served as the territory's Commissioner of Finance.

According to the Wikipedia "List of people named in the Epstein files," emails in the files show that from 2008 to 2009 Jeffrey Epstein paid tuition for de Jongh's children. The Elon News Network, reporting on Department of Justice files, states that a tuition request connected to Epstein totaled $70,066 for students attending American University, Elon University, Skidmore College, and Wake Forest University, and that de Jongh's wife, Cecile de Jongh, asked Epstein to cover tuition for her two children and for two children of Jeanne Brennan Wiebracht, the governor's former campaign accountant. The Elon News Network quotes Cecile de Jongh describing the arrangement as "a treat," writing, "Its as if i had my own kids in college."

Much of the reporting centers on Cecile de Jongh, who served as First Lady during her husband's terms. HuffPost reported that JPMorgan Chase alleged in a 2023 court filing that Epstein "paid the school tuition for her and the governor's children" and that Cecile de Jongh acted as Epstein's "primary conduit" within the territory's government — claims made in litigation and not findings of wrongdoing against John de Jongh. HuffPost noted that de Jongh "has not returned media inquiries" about the allegations and that a spokesperson for the Virgin Islands attorney general called JPMorgan's filing "an obvious attempt to shift blame."

## Is John de Jongh Jr. in the Epstein files?

Yes. John de Jongh Jr., former Governor of the U.S. Virgin Islands, is named in the Epstein files in connection with college tuition that Jeffrey Epstein paid for his children in 2008–2009, as documented in the Wikipedia list of people named in the files and in reporting by the Elon News Network on Department of Justice files.
