Lisa Randall is a theoretical physicist and the Frank B. Baird, Jr. Professor of Science at Harvard University, where her research spans particle physics, cosmology, extra dimensions, and dark matter. According to her Wikipedia biography, she co-developed the Randall–Sundrum model, was the first tenured female theoretical physicist at Harvard, and received the J.J. Sakurai Prize for Theoretical Particle Physics in 2019.

According to The Harvard Crimson, Randall's connection to [Jeffrey Epstein](/people/jeffrey-epstein) began in 2004 at a dinner hosted by literary agent John Brockman, and she attended a gravity conference in St. Thomas in 2006 that Epstein sponsored. The Crimson reported that she maintained contact with him for nearly a decade — including emails, phone calls, and in-person meetings in Cambridge — continuing years after his 2008 Florida conviction. The Boston Globe, reviewing the same Justice Department documents, described the exchanges as casual and friendly, quoting messages in which Randall asked "What does fun consist of? For you?" and joked "Wow haven't you had enough house arrest?" about his legal situation. The Globe documented email dates including October 2009, June and July 2010, August 2010, November 2014, and 2017.

The Harvard Crimson reported that on November 30, 2014, Randall briefly visited Epstein's Caribbean island, traveling there by boat from St. John around noon and departing by helicopter that afternoon, before flying on Epstein's private jet from the U.S. Virgin Islands to Boston. According to the Crimson, fellow passengers on that flight included former MIT Media Lab director Joichi Ito, LinkedIn co-founder Reid Hoffman, and others. Asked about the contact, Randall stated, as quoted by The Boston Globe: "I am appalled by the crimes that were revealed and the terrible harm he caused his victims. I deeply regret maintaining contact." The Globe noted she also said she never received research funding from him.

## Is Lisa Randall in the Epstein files?

Yes. Both outlets reported that she has not been accused of any wrongdoing, that she said she never received research funding from Epstein, and that she stated she deeply regrets maintaining contact.
