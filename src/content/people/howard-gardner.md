Howard Gardner is a developmental psychologist at Harvard University, where, according to his Wikipedia biography, he holds the position of John H. and Elisabeth A. Hobbs Research Professor of Cognition and Education and was a founding member of Harvard Project Zero. He is best known for his theory of multiple intelligences, set out in his 1983 book *Frames of Mind: The Theory of Multiple Intelligences*, which proposes that people process information through several relatively independent capacities.

Gardner's documented connection to [Jeffrey Epstein](/people/jeffrey-epstein) arises from reporting on Epstein's relationships with academics rather than from a court filing or named-defendant list. His Wikipedia biography states that "Gardner knew sex offender Jeffrey Epstein, who supported some of Gardner's research at Harvard," and that "after Epstein's arrest in 2006, Gardner told Epstein that he could not accept any more donations from him." BuzzFeed News reported that Gardner, described as a Harvard specialist in cognitive science and education, thanked Epstein in a 2005 book for research support and "valued friendship." Gardner's connection sits within a broader pattern documented by Harvard: the university said it received about $9.1 million in gifts from Epstein between 1998 and 2007 and accepted no donations from him after his 2008 conviction.

Gardner has stated that he ended financial ties with Epstein. He told BuzzFeed News by email, "I told JE explicitly that I could not and would not accept any further support from him." Asked about a photograph said to place him at a 2012 meeting attended by Epstein, Gardner told BuzzFeed News, "I have no memory of this." The Harvard report cited here does not name Gardner, and he does not appear on the Wikipedia "List of people named in the Epstein files" that was reviewed for this profile.
