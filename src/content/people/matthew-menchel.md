According to the Wikipedia "List of people named in the Epstein files," Matthew Menchel was the chief criminal prosecutor who spearheaded Epstein's 2007 plea deal, after which he left the Department of Justice and continued to be in contact with Epstein. The same entry states that they exchanged emails between 2010 and 2018 and shared multiple dinners and meetings.

The ABA Journal, reporting on documents released as part of the Epstein files, describes Menchel as the former chief of the criminal division in the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of Florida, and reports that he "left his job as prosecutor in August 2007 to join the international law firm Kobre & Kim." According to the ABA Journal, the meetings and dinners between Menchel and Epstein "happened in 2011, 2013 and 2017," beginning four years after he left his post. Kobre & Kim's own firm biography confirms that, before joining the firm, Menchel "was a prosecutor at the U.S. Department of Justice (as an Assistant U.S. Attorney in the Criminal Division of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of Florida), where he was also chief of the Criminal Division."

Menchel has disputed the characterization of his relationship with Epstein. As quoted by Yahoo News, he stated: "I had no business relationship with Mr. Epstein at any point, not before, during or after my tenure at the US Attorney's Office," adding that when Epstein's team asked his firm to handle litigation, "we declined." According to that report, after publication Menchel told the Miami Herald that he never skied with Epstein, but did not deny that he had met with Epstein after leaving the U.S. Attorney's Office.
