David Copperfield is an American stage magician and illusionist, born in 1956. According to his Wikipedia biography, he is described by *Forbes* as the most commercially successful magician in history, and over a career spanning more than four decades he has won 21 Emmy Awards, holds multiple Guinness World Records, and is known for large-scale illusions such as appearing to make the Statue of Liberty disappear.

Copperfield's name appears in reporting and documents connected to Jeffrey Epstein. According to the Wikipedia article "List of people named in the Epstein files," phone-message pads from 2005 indicated that Copperfield left messages for Epstein, and an October 2007 email described him as "Epstein's favorite cohort" and stated that backstage passes to his performances were given as gifts to girls. The same article notes that Johanna Sjoberg testified to attending a 2004 dinner at Epstein's home where Copperfield performed magic tricks, and that the records include photographs of Copperfield with Ghislaine Maxwell. The Daily Beast reported that Sjoberg met Copperfield at a dinner party at Epstein's home in 2004. According to Yahoo's reporting, his name appeared in a batch of so-called Epstein files released by the U.S. Justice Department, and the same article references an FBI characterization of a "very close relationship" between the two men.

Copperfield has, through his attorneys, disputed the characterization of his association with Epstein. The Daily Beast quoted his attorneys as saying the reporting was "a total distortion of the facts" and that "David and Jeffrey Epstein were, at most, acquaintances" who "only ever met on a handful of occasions." Yahoo reported a further statement from his attorneys that "our client did not know about Epstein's horrific crimes" and that, "like the rest of the world, he learned about it from the press." The Wikipedia list entry likewise records that Copperfield denied the friendship.
