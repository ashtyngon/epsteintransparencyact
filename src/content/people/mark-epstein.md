Mark Epstein, born in 1954, is the younger brother of the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. According to Wikipedia, he is a property developer and real estate investor who founded the firm Ossa Properties and the venture Dara Partners, alongside other business interests. His relationship to Jeffrey Epstein is familial: he is identified in reference sources simply as Jeffrey Epstein's brother rather than as a business partner or associate in his brother's affairs.

Mark Epstein is best known publicly for questioning the official account of his brother's death. Jeffrey Epstein was found dead in a federal jail cell in August 2019; the New York City medical examiner ruled the death a suicide by hanging, a conclusion later supported by the FBI and, in June 2023, affirmed by the U.S. Department of Justice Inspector General after a review the agency described as covering dozens of witness interviews and roughly 100,000 documents, per the Wikipedia article "Death of Jeffrey Epstein." Mark Epstein has publicly rejected the suicide finding. According to his Wikipedia profile, he has said the death "looks more like a homicide" and has urged authorities to reopen the investigation.

He has continued to raise these doubts in interviews. Newsweek reported that in July 2025 Mark Epstein appeared on the BBC program *Newsnight*, where "he argued the New York financier did not commit suicide in his jail cell in 2019," and that he repeated his doubts in a separate interview with Al Arabiya English. In the same Newsweek account, Mark Epstein described his last conversation with his brother as occurring the night before Jeffrey Epstein's July 2019 arrest, calling it "just a normal brother-to-brother" call, and said, "I did not speak to Jeffrey after he was arrested." These official rulings stand notwithstanding his public statements, and the sources reviewed here do not establish that his doubts have changed the formal cause-of-death determination.

## Is Mark Epstein in the Epstein files?

Mark Epstein appears on Wikipedia's "List of people named in the Epstein files" only in its personal/family section, where he is listed as "Mark Epstein (property developer) (brother)." He is identified there strictly as Jeffrey Epstein's brother, not as a client, associate, or participant in the conduct at the center of the Epstein investigations. His public relevance derives from his family relationship to Jeffrey Epstein and from his role as a vocal critic of the official conclusion that his brother died by suicide, as described in the sources above. The materials reviewed for this profile do not contain allegations of wrongdoing against Mark Epstein.
