Mette-Marit (full name Mette-Marit Tjessem Høiby) is the Crown Princess of Norway. According to her Wikipedia biography, she became Crown Princess on 25 August 2001 upon her marriage to Haakon, Crown Prince of Norway, the heir apparent to the Norwegian throne.

According to Wikipedia's "List of people named in the Epstein files," Mette-Marit is mentioned over a thousand times in the files, with communications spanning 2011 to 2014. A dedicated Wikipedia article on the relationship reports that the two maintained close contact until 2014, met several times, and that she stayed in Epstein's later-demolished Palm Beach mansion in 2013. That article quotes emails attributed to her, including a 2011 message reading "Googled u after last email[.] Agree it didn't look too good :)" and 2012 messages such as "You tickle my brain" and "come and save us. I die of boredom." Both Wikipedia entries state the relevant Epstein files were released in late January 2026.

According to Marie Claire (February 6, 2026), the Norwegian royal household issued a statement that said: "We understand the strong reactions people have to what has emerged in recent days. The Crown Princess strongly disavows Epstein's abuse and criminal acts. She is very sorry for not having understood early enough what kind of person he was." In her own statement, reported by the same outlet, Mette-Marit said: "I would like to express my deepest regret for my friendship with Jeffrey Epstein," and added that "some of the content of the messages between Epstein and me does not represent the person I want to be."
