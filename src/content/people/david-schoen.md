David Schoen is an American attorney whose practice has focused on federal criminal defense and civil-rights litigation. His work has spanned civil-rights cases challenging police and prison conduct, voting-rights and ballot-access matters, and other civil litigation, and his clients have included Roger Stone and Steve Bannon. He gained wider public attention representing former President Donald Trump during Trump's second impeachment trial in 2021. He also "briefly" represented Jeffrey Epstein before Epstein's death.

In interviews cited by Newsweek, Schoen has described how he came to be involved with Epstein. He has said he first met Epstein around 2008, during Epstein's earlier case, and that he was in contact with Epstein again in the summer of 2019 while Epstein was held at the Metropolitan Correctional Center in Manhattan on new federal charges. Schoen told Newsweek: "I met with Mr. Epstein on August 1st at his request. We met for 5 hours," and said that during that period Epstein asked him to take over his defense. According to Schoen, "The purpose of asking me to come there that day and over the past previous couple of weeks was to ask me to take over his defence. We came to an agreement during the course of that discussion."

Schoen has also publicly questioned the official determination that Epstein died by suicide. He has said Epstein appeared "animated and energized" after their meeting and eager to fight the case, and stated, "I don't think it was a suicide." Schoen has said he had arranged to meet Epstein again around the time of Epstein's death but postponed the meeting. Epstein died at the Manhattan jail in August 2019 in what was officially ruled a suicide.
