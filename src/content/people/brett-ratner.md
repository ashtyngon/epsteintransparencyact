Brett Ratner (born March 28, 1969) is an American film director and producer. He is known for the *Rush Hour* film series, *The Family Man*, *Red Dragon*, *X-Men: The Last Stand*, *Tower Heist*, and *Hercules*. More recently, according to Deadline, he directed the Amazon MGM Studios documentary *Melania*, focused on First Lady Melania Trump in the days before the 2025 inauguration.

Ratner's connection to the Jeffrey Epstein record stems from photographs released as part of the Epstein files. According to reporting on the released files, a photo released in December 2025 showed Ratner posing with Jean-Luc Brunel, an associate of Epstein, and further images released in January 2026 showed Ratner with Epstein and young women. Fox News reported in February 2026 that documents released by the Department of Justice included "an image of Ratner with his arm around an unidentified woman sitting next to Epstein," in a photo also described as showing Ratner, Epstein, and Brunel alongside several women whose identities have not been disclosed.

Ratner has publicly disputed any relationship with Epstein. He told Fox News Digital that the photograph was taken roughly 20 years ago at an event he attended with the woman, whom he described as his fiancée at the time: "We went to an event, and we were sitting on the couch, and the photo was taken at that event." He added, "I didn't have a personal relationship with him. I didn't know him," and called the speculation "crazy" and "horrible." Deadline reported that "there is no suggestion that his inclusion in the Epstein files means Ratner was aware of the financier's wrongdoing."
