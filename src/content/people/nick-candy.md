Nick Candy is a British luxury property developer and businessman, born in 1973, who co-founded the interior design and development firm Candy & Candy in 1999 and, in 2004, the CPC Group with his brother Christian, according to his Wikipedia biography. The brothers are associated with high-end London developments, most prominently the One Hyde Park scheme in Knightsbridge. Wikipedia states that Candy was appointed treasurer of Reform UK by Nigel Farage in December 2024, after he left the Conservative Party.

Candy is named in materials released from the Epstein files. Wikipedia's "List of people named in the Epstein files" states: "Nick Candy is a luxury property developer who serves as treasurer for Reform UK. Candy interacted with Epstein associates Sarah Kellen, Ghislaine Maxwell and Jed Garfield." Reporting by Middle East Eye (Imran Mulla, February 3, 2026) and Left Foot Forward (Olivia Barber, February 2026) describes emails dating to 2004 in which Candy was in contact with Sarah Kellen — a central figure in Epstein's circle — and sought the email address and phone number of Ghislaine Maxwell, who is now serving a 20-year US prison sentence for child sex trafficking. According to Left Foot Forward, Candy wrote that he would "be in New York for four days starting Thursday 9 December," and the same report references a 2010 dealing in which a One Hyde Park property was offered to Epstein for £80 million. Both outlets emphasize that the documented material does not establish any crime: Middle East Eye states "There is no suggestion of wrongdoing on Candy's part," and Left Foot Forward writes that "The files prove no criminal wrongdoing, but do show Candy had contacts within Epstein's network."

None of the sources reviewed for this profile quoted a direct statement from Candy himself responding to the files. Candy's Wikipedia biography reports that "Nigel Farage said he had 'no concerns' about Candy's appearance in the Epstein files." Middle East Eye reported that it had contacted Reform and invited both the party and Candy to comment.
