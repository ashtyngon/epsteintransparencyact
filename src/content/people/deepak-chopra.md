Deepak Chopra is an American author, public speaker, and alternative-medicine advocate, born October 22, 1946, in New Delhi. According to his Wikipedia biography, he is a prominent figure in the New Age movement whose books and videos have made him one of the best-known and wealthiest figures in alternative medicine; he is widely associated with concepts such as "quantum healing," an approach that medical and scientific commentators have criticized as pseudoscience. He earned his medical degree from the All India Institute of Medical Sciences and emigrated to the United States in 1970.

Chopra's name appears in material connected to the release of records relating to Jeffrey Epstein. Wikipedia's "List of people named in the Epstein files" describes Chopra as a "New Age guru" and states that he "met with Epstein at least a dozen times between 2016 and 2019," adding that the University of California, San Diego ended his affiliation with the school. Reporting on the documents released by the U.S. Department of Justice, ThePrint and Complex describe email exchanges between Chopra and Epstein dated around 2017, including invitations to events and trips. According to those accounts, the correspondence included messages in which Chopra urged Epstein to "bring your girls" on a trip and a line reading "God is a construct. Cute girls are real." These outlets present the material as released correspondence rather than as evidence of any crime by Chopra.

In a public statement quoted by Complex and ThePrint, Chopra responded to the released emails. He said: "I was never involved in, nor did I participate in, any criminal or exploitative conduct," and that "Any contact I had was limited and unrelated to abusive activity." He also acknowledged that "Some past email exchanges have surfaced that reflect poor judgment in tone," adding, "I regret that and understand how they read today, given what was publicly known at the time." His Wikipedia biography similarly notes that, according to documents from the Epstein files, Chopra had multiple meetings and email exchanges with Epstein and that he has denied any wrongdoing.

## Is Deepak Chopra in the Epstein files?

Yes. Based on the sources reviewed for this profile, Deepak Chopra is named in records connected to Jeffrey Epstein. Wikipedia's list of people named in the Epstein files states that he met with Epstein at least a dozen times between 2016 and 2019, and reporting by ThePrint and Complex describes email exchanges between the two — released as part of the U.S. Department of Justice's Epstein materials — that include messages from around 2017. None of the sources reviewed here state that Chopra has been accused of, charged with, or convicted of any crime in connection with Epstein. Chopra has publicly denied any criminal or exploitative conduct while acknowledging that some past email exchanges reflected "poor judgment in tone." Being named in these documents is not, by itself, evidence of wrongdoing.
