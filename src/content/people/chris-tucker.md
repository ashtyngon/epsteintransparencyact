Christopher Tucker (born August 31, 1971) is an American stand-up comedian and actor. He gained international prominence through the *Rush Hour* film series alongside Jackie Chan, in which he played Detective James Carter, and also appeared in films including *Friday* (1995) and *The Fifth Element* (1997).

Tucker's connection to the Jeffrey Epstein record is a single documented trip. On a high-profile trip in 2002, Clinton flew aboard Epstein's jet to Africa, along with actors Kevin Spacey and Chris Tucker, to visit HIV/AIDS project sites. FactCheck.org dates the journey to "Sept. 21, 2002," describing it as "a nine-day trip to Africa with actors Kevin Spacey and Chris Tucker, visiting Ghana, Nigeria, Rwanda, Mozambique and South Africa," during which Clinton "worked on HIV and AIDS prevention projects, democratization, and economic development."

FactCheck.org reports that this Africa trip "was the longest trip Clinton took on Epstein's plane, and it accounted for 11 of the 26 total flights" logged on Epstein's aircraft between February 2002 and November 2003. The reporting frames the flight in the context of Clinton Foundation humanitarian work, and it predates the public emergence of the criminal allegations against Epstein.
