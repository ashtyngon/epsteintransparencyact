Børge Brende is a Norwegian politician who served as Norway's Minister of Foreign Affairs from 2013 to 2017 and earlier held cabinet posts including Minister of the Environment and Minister of Trade and Industry, according to his Wikipedia biography. In October 2017 he became President and CEO of the World Economic Forum, the organization that convenes the annual Davos meeting, a role he held until February 2026.

Brende is listed among people named in reporting on the Jeffrey Epstein files. According to Wikipedia's "List of people named in the Epstein files," Brende exchanged more than 120 emails and text messages with Epstein and was a guest at several dinners at Epstein's townhouse in New York City; the entry adds that a photograph shows Brende at Epstein's New York home alongside Steve Bannon and Terje Rød-Larsen, and that Brende said he was introduced to Epstein by Rød-Larsen.

Brende's own account differs in scale from the figures cited above. According to Al Jazeera, in his resignation statement Brende said he had three business dinners with Epstein — one in 2018 and two in 2019 — and exchanged "a few emails and SMS messages," which he described as "the extent of my interactions with him." Al Jazeera reported that Brende said he was "completely unaware of Epstein's past and criminal activities" and expressed regret for not investigating Epstein's background more thoroughly. The World Economic Forum opened an independent review of the relationship conducted by outside counsel; per Al Jazeera, co-chairs André Hoffmann and Larry Fink said the review found no additional concerns beyond what had previously been disclosed. Brende resigned as the Forum's president and CEO on February 26, 2026, and Alois Zwinggi was named interim president and CEO.
