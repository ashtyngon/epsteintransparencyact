Ben Goertzel is an American computer scientist and artificial-intelligence researcher. According to his Wikipedia article, he was born on December 8, 1966, earned a PhD in mathematics from Temple University in 1990, and helped popularize the term "artificial general intelligence" (AGI). He is the founder and CEO of SingularityNET, a blockchain-based AI project, and a leading developer of the OpenCog framework; he previously served as chief scientist of Hanson Robotics, the Hong Kong-based company that created the Sophia robot.

Goertzel's documented appearance in connection with [Jeffrey Epstein](/people/jeffrey-epstein) concerns research funding. According to the Wikipedia article on people named in the Epstein files, "between 2010 and 2015, Epstein's non-profits channeled at least $113,000 to Goertzel's company," a step that "made Goertzel eligible to secure over $1 million in grants from the Hong Kong government," and the two "corresponded between 2011 and at least through 2018." The South China Morning Post, in a February 16, 2026 investigation by Connor Mycroft, reported that Epstein provided at least US$113,000 to Goertzel's open-source AI work over at least five years from 2010 to 2015 and that Goertzel went on to secure at least HK$8.9 million in Hong Kong government grants at a city university. The Anadolu Agency, in a February 16, 2026 report by Berk Kutay Gokmen, reported that the funding was channeled through Humanity+, a US non-profit, and then transferred to Novamente, a company owned by Goertzel; that Epstein's support began in 2001 with a research fellowship; and that the funding was suspended in 2015 amid renewed scrutiny of Epstein.

In response, according to the Anadolu Agency report, Goertzel said: "Looking back, I regret knowing the guy, or taking his money, or having anything to do with the guy," adding that "getting research money is hard" and that "it's hard to say no" when facing funding shortages. The South China Morning Post stated that "there is no evidence that connects Goertzel with the illegal acts committed by Epstein."
