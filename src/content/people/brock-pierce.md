Brock Pierce (born November 14, 1980) is an American cryptocurrency entrepreneur and former child actor. According to his Wikipedia entry, he acted in Disney films including *The Mighty Ducks* (1992) and *First Kid* (1996) before leaving acting, then built a career in digital entertainment and cryptocurrency. He co-founded Tether in 2014, founded Blockchain Capital in 2013, and co-founded Block.one, which released the EOS.IO software. He ran as an independent candidate in the 2020 US presidential election.

In February 2026, several outlets reported that emails between Pierce and [Jeffrey Epstein](/people/jeffrey-epstein) appeared in newly released US Department of Justice documents. The crypto news outlet *Protos* (February 2, 2026) reported that Pierce and Epstein first connected at the 2011 Mindshift Conference held on Epstein's private island, Little Saint James, where Pierce's cryptocurrency presentation drew Epstein's attention. *Protos* and *Fortune* (February 6, 2026) both reported that, as a managing partner at Crypto Currency Partners — later rebranded Blockchain Capital — Pierce advised Epstein on crypto investments, and that in late 2014 Pierce contacted Epstein about investing in Coinbase's Series C round. *CoinDesk* (published February 4, 2026) reported that, per the files, Pierce wrote to Epstein on December 2, 2014, describing the Coinbase opportunity as "the most platinum-plated deal in the space," and that Epstein invested roughly $3 million through a US Virgin Islands entity. *Protos* and *Fortune* reported that Epstein later sold part of that stake in 2018.

*Protos* also reported that, in a May 2012 episode described in the files, Epstein asked Pierce during a trip to "Moscow, Kiev, and Odessa" to "take photos and find me a present," after which Pierce emailed Epstein photographs of a Ukrainian woman named Anastasia, writing that "Ukraine is now my favorite country." Regarding any response, *Fortune* reported that Pierce, who left Blockchain Capital in 2017, "did not immediately respond to a request for comment," and *Protos* said it reached out to Pierce for comment without a published reply. A Blockchain Capital spokesperson told *Fortune* that "Mr. Epstein has never been an investor in any Blockchain Capital fund." *Fortune* stated that "neither Pierce, Blockchain Capital, nor Coinbase have been linked to any wrongdoing with Epstein."
