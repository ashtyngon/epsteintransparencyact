William Joseph Burns (born 1956) is an American career diplomat. According to his Wikipedia biography, he served as Director of the Central Intelligence Agency from March 2021 to January 2025 and as U.S. Deputy Secretary of State from 2011 to 2014, after a Foreign Service career that included postings as U.S. Ambassador to Jordan (1998–2001) and to Russia (2005–2008) and as Under Secretary of State for Political Affairs (2008–2011).

According to The Wall Street Journal's 2023 reporting, as summarized by Wikipedia and The Daily Beast, scheduling and calendar documents in Epstein's records showed three meetings arranged with Jeffrey Epstein in 2014, while Burns was Deputy Secretary of State. Wikipedia's account of the WSJ reporting describes a lunch scheduled in August 2014 at a Washington law firm office and two evening appointments scheduled in September 2014 at Epstein's Manhattan townhouse, and states that after one meeting Epstein planned for his driver to take Burns to the airport.

The Daily Beast reported a statement from a CIA spokesperson that Burns "did not know anything about [Epstein], other than that he was introduced as an expert in the financial services sector and offered general advice on transition to the private sector," and that "they had no relationship." The Wikipedia entry on people named in the Epstein files adds that the CIA denied Burns had any knowledge of Epstein's personal life, that Burns recalled meeting Epstein briefly through a mutual friend in Washington while denying the other scheduled meetings, and that he said he regretted the meetings.
