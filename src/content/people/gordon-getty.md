Gordon Peter Getty (born December 20, 1933) is an American businessman and classical music composer, the fourth child of oil tycoon J. Paul Getty. He assumed control of the family's multibillion-dollar trust in 1976 and oversaw the 1986 sale of Getty Oil to Texaco. Alongside his business career he is an accomplished classical composer of opera, choral, and chamber works, including *Plump Jack*, *Usher House*, and *The Canterville Ghost*.

Reporting on the Epstein files indicates that the evolutionary scientist Robert Trivers — a longtime friend of both men — introduced Getty, the billionaire heir of the Getty Oil fortune, to Jeffrey Epstein in 2013, after which the two corresponded and spent time together from 2013 to 2018. The released files document that the email exchanges began in 2013, five years after Epstein's 2008 Florida conviction. They include a May 3, 2018 email in which Trivers told Epstein that Getty had asked "whether you were the same Jeffrey accused of relations with minors," and that on being told yes, Getty replied, "well, we ALL have similar kinds of problems" — a remark Trivers framed as a reference to Getty's own publicly reported scandal involving three daughters fathered in an extramarital relationship. According to reporting on the released files, the two remained on email threads until April 2019, though it is unclear whether personal communication continued.

Reporting by Meaww (published November 2025) independently notes that Getty's emails appeared in material released by the House Oversight Committee, and that around March 2018 Getty used the correspondence to argue that the United States faced "a different and deeper crisis" of presidential temperament. None of the sources reviewed for this profile included any public response, comment, or denial from Gordon Getty or his representatives regarding the emails; that material is omitted here pending verification.
