Nathan Wolfe is an American virologist known for his work on pandemic prediction and prevention. He founded the Global Viral Forecasting Initiative (later Global Viral) and the firm Metabiota, conducted years of biomedical research in sub-Saharan Africa and Southeast Asia, and authored the 2011 book *The Viral Storm: The Dawn of a New Pandemic Age*. The Stanford Daily reports that he was formerly the Lorry I. Lokey Visiting Professor in Human Biology at Stanford and that his LinkedIn profile describes him as a Visiting Scholar at the Quake Lab in Stanford's Department of Bioengineering.

The Stanford Daily reported on February 3, 2026 that Wolfe's full name appears 589 times in the released Epstein files, describing "friendly communications between the two men from 2008–2013." According to the same reporting, in December 2010 Wolfe invited Epstein to a dinner, and in May 2013 he proposed a study linking "sexual behavior and microbial diversity" — a project he referred to as a "horny virus hypothesis" — explicitly raising Epstein's potential funding for it. The Stanford Daily also reported that Wolfe's 2012 book referenced Epstein as a "loyal and generous" friend. The correspondence postdated Epstein's 2008 guilty plea to state charges.

In a statement reported by The Stanford Daily on February 5, 2026, Wolfe denied any wrongdoing, said the research project was never pursued and that he never received funding from Epstein, and stated that he met Epstein professionally at his New York and Palm Beach homes but never visited his island or flew on his plane. He acknowledged "overfamiliarity and poor judgment" in the correspondence and said, "I never witnessed or participated in any misconduct or inappropriate behavior," adding, "I deeply regret having had any association with someone responsible for such widespread abuse."
