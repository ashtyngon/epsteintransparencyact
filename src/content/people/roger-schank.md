Roger Schank (1946–2023) was an American artificial-intelligence theorist, cognitive psychologist, learning scientist and educational reformer. According to his Wikipedia biography, he pioneered conceptual dependency theory for natural-language understanding in 1969, helped develop case-based reasoning, and introduced the "scripts, plans and goals" framework for modeling human knowledge. He held positions at Stanford and Yale (where he worked from 1974 to 1989) and in 1989 founded the Institute for the Learning Sciences at Northwestern University.

His Wikipedia biography states that, in Florida, Schank "was a neighbor of Jeffrey Epstein, and attended an artificial intelligence conference sponsored by him on his island in 2002," six years before Epstein was convicted of a sex offense in 2008. The same biography reports that "after the conviction, Schank showed public support for him." It cites coverage by Slate (2019) and New York Magazine's Intelligencer (2008) for this period, alongside later 2026 reporting by The Daily Northwestern.

The Wikipedia biography further states that "Epstein files were released in 2026 that contained emails from Schank that are widely considered to be deeply misogynist," describing derogatory statements about women's intelligence, sexually explicit language, and disparagement of other experts. An independent tracker, the Epstein Web project, similarly describes Schank as a Palm Beach neighbor and email correspondent of Epstein and notes continuing contact around 2009 after Epstein's Florida case. Schank died in 2023; the released correspondence and reporting concerning it are posthumous.
