Vitaly Ivanovich Churkin was a Russian career diplomat. He was born in Moscow on February 21, 1952, and served as Russia's Permanent Representative to the United Nations from May 1, 2006, until his death on February 20, 2017. Before the UN posting, he held several ambassadorial roles, including Ambassador to Belgium and Ambassador to Canada, and served as a Deputy Foreign Minister. He died in New York City at the age of 64.

Churkin is named in connection with the released Epstein files. Reporting on the files indicates they suggested he regularly met with Epstein and that Epstein offered to help Churkin's son Maxim find work at a wealth management firm.
