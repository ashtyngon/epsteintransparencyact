Bill Richardson (1947–2023) was a U.S. Democratic politician with a long career in public service. According to his Wikipedia biography, he represented New Mexico's 3rd congressional district from 1983 to 1997, served as U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations from 1997 to 1998, as U.S. Secretary of Energy from 1998 to 2001, and as the 30th Governor of New Mexico from 2003 to 2011. He died on September 1, 2023.

Richardson's name appears in records connected to Jeffrey Epstein. According to the Santa Fe New Mexican, documents released by the U.S. Department of Justice show that Richardson arranged to meet with Epstein on several occasions in the years after Epstein's 2006 Florida conviction, with contacts documented as late as 2018. The same reporting notes that Richardson returned, by donating to charity, campaign contributions Epstein had made to his gubernatorial campaigns.

Court documents unsealed in August 2019 — part of a settled defamation lawsuit between Virginia Giuffre and Ghislaine Maxwell — included Richardson's name among individuals Giuffre alleged in a 2016 deposition that she was directed to have contact with. As CBS News reported, Richardson denied the allegations, issuing a statement that called them "completely false" and stating that he had "never met Ms. Giuffre." According to his Wikipedia biography, his attorney said federal prosecutors had informed them that Richardson was "neither a target, subject, nor witness" in their investigation. Richardson was never charged in connection with Epstein.
