George McDonald Church is the Robert Winthrop Professor of Genetics at Harvard Medical School and a professor of health sciences and technology at Harvard and MIT, as well as a founding member of the Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering, according to [his Wikipedia biography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Church_(geneticist)). Born August 28, 1954, he is a pioneer in genomics and synthetic biology: the same source notes he co-authored an early direct genomic sequencing method in 1984, helped launch the Human Genome Project, and has had a laboratory that spun off dozens of biotechnology companies. He drew scrutiny in connection with [Jeffrey Epstein](/people/jeffrey-epstein) over research funding and meetings, for which he publicly apologized in 2019.

## Connection to Epstein

According to the Wikipedia [list of people named in the Epstein files](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_people_named_in_the_Epstein_files), Church — identified there as a Harvard genetics professor — met with Epstein multiple times in 2014 and dined with him alongside Joi Ito, Reid Hoffman, and Martin Nowak; the entry adds that Church apologized in 2019, saying he was unaware of Epstein's crimes, and that later reporting contradicted this. In an August 2019 interview with [STAT News](https://www.statnews.com/2019/08/05/citing-nerd-tunnel-vision-biologist-george-church-apologizes-for-contacts-with-jeffrey-epstein/), Church said Epstein had contributed unrestricted funding to his lab from 2005 to 2007 for early-stage projects, and he confirmed having six phone calls and meetings with Epstein in 2014 — including a calendar entry he described as a June 21, 2014 lunch with Epstein at Martin Nowak's institute — and said he met with Epstein several times in years since. Church's Wikipedia biography states that, after Epstein's 2008 conviction, then-Harvard president Drew Gilpin Faust prohibited the university from accepting further donations from him.

## Public statements

Church told STAT News, "I certainly apologize for my poor awareness and judgment," and reflected that scientists "should have been more conversations about, should we be doing this, should we be helping this guy? There was just a lot of nerd tunnel vision." He said he had read "a couple of news articles" about Epstein's 2008 conviction but that they "weren't clear enough for me to know there was a serious problem," and, asked about Epstein's reported eugenic ambitions, said, "I never heard anything about it." In a December 2019 interview with [60 Minutes](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/harvard-scientist-george-church-talks-about-accepting-donations-from-jeffrey-epstein-60-minutes-2019-12-08/), Church said he had met Epstein "at meetings with other scientists," called the association "unfortunate," and, asked whether he regretted taking the money, replied, "I regret not knowing more about the donor," adding that "you don't always know your donors as well as you would like."

## Is George Church in the Epstein files?

Yes. George Church is named in the Wikipedia [list of people named in the Epstein files](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_people_named_in_the_Epstein_files), which documents that records showed he met with Epstein multiple times in 2014 and dined with him alongside Joi Ito, Reid Hoffman, and Martin Nowak. His lab received research funding from Epstein between 2005 and 2007, and Church publicly apologized in 2019 for his contacts with Epstein, telling [STAT News](https://www.statnews.com/2019/08/05/citing-nerd-tunnel-vision-biologist-george-church-apologizes-for-contacts-with-jeffrey-epstein/) that he apologized for his "poor awareness and judgment." Being named in these records is not in itself evidence of wrongdoing; the documented connection is the research funding and the meetings, which Church has acknowledged and addressed publicly.
