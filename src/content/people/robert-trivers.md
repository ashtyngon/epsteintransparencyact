Robert Trivers was an American evolutionary biologist, born February 19, 1943, who profoundly influenced evolutionary biology and sociobiology through theories including reciprocal altruism, parental investment, parent-offspring conflict, and self-deception. He held faculty positions at Harvard University, the University of California, Santa Cruz, and Rutgers University (1994–2015), was awarded the 2007 Crafoord Prize in Biosciences, and died March 12, 2026. Coverage by the New York Daily News (via Yahoo News, February 3, 2026) describes him as an "ex-Rutgers professor" who served as president of the Biosocial Research Foundation, based in Millstone, New Jersey, and reports that he was suspended by Rutgers in 2014 and later received a buyout.

Inside Higher Ed reported on February 1, 2015 that Trivers, then described as a Rutgers University biologist, received approximately $40,000 from [Jeffrey Epstein](/people/jeffrey-epstein) to study the link between knee symmetry and sprinting ability. In the same period, Inside Higher Ed quoted Trivers questioning the severity of the charges against Epstein, saying: "By the time they're 14 or 15, they're like grown women were 60 years ago, so I don't see these acts as so heinous." Inside Higher Ed said it contacted both Trivers and Rutgers for further comment and received no response. The New York Daily News reported in February 2026, citing released emails, that Epstein wrote a $30,000 check to Trivers's foundation and that Trivers confirmed receiving roughly $40,000 from Epstein for the knee-symmetry research; it also noted Trivers had called Epstein "a person of integrity" in a 2015 Reuters interview.

A separate Inside Higher Ed article from February 3, 2026 ("9 More Higher Ed Names in the Epstein Files") reported additional quotes attributed to Trivers from released emails, including that Epstein "was always a gentleman" and a message in which Trivers wrote that "the basic problem is simple—i am going against a strong national trend—well-known man after well-known man is being brought low for alleged his misbehaviour toward women." The Epstein files released in 2026 record that Trivers, a longtime friend of both financier Gordon Getty and Jeffrey Epstein, introduced Getty to Epstein in 2013, and that Getty often funded Trivers's research. Reporting on the released files also attributes a 2017 statement to Trivers describing his relationship with Epstein as "valuable mostly because he is extremely bright, open-minded and widely travelled."
