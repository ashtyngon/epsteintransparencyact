The Department of Justice plans to release nearly 50,000 additional documents related to Jeffrey Epstein, according to Newsradio WOOD 1300 and 106.9 FM.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The 50,000-document figure would represent one of the largest single releases of Epstein-related materials to date.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Previous disclosures have included court filings from civil litigation, visitor logs, and investigative materials.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The disclosure follows the enactment of the [Epstein Transparency Act](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law), which mandated the release of government documents related to Epstein's case and his associates.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The FBI [began its review of Epstein-related records](/news/fbi-begins-epstein-records-review) under the new law earlier this year.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Epstein Transparency Act passed both chambers of Congress with bipartisan support and requires executive branch agencies to review and declassify documents related to Epstein within specific timeframes.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Congressional Investigations Continue

The DOJ's planned release follows active congressional investigations into Epstein's network of associates. A House committee recently [subpoenaed Bill Gates and former Obama White House counsel Kathryn Ruemmler](/news/2026-03-04-house-panel-subpoenas-bill-gates-kathryn-ruemmler-in-epstein-probe) as part of its probe.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick is scheduled to [testify about a visit to Epstein's private island](/news/2026-03-04-commerce-secretary-lutnick-to-testify-on-epstein-island-visit).<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> [Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker responded](/news/2026-03-04-gov-pritzker-responds-after-clinton-names-him-in-epstein-deposition) after being named in a deposition involving former President Bill Clinton.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Representative Ro Khanna (D-Calif.) stated in February that he expects [more names will emerge from the Epstein files](/news/2026-02-28-rep-ro-khanna-more-names-will-emerge-from-epstein-files) as the review and release process continues under the transparency law.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

A Wall Street Journal investigation reported in February that the [Department of Justice examined several Epstein associates for potential sex crimes but charged none](/news/2026-03-01-wsj-doj-examined-epstein-associates-for-sex-crimes-charged-none).<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Public Analysis of Records

As document releases have accelerated in recent months, [citizen journalists have taken on a role in analyzing the materials](/news/2026-03-01-citizen-journalists-decode-epstein-documents-as-public-release-accelerates), working to decode references and identify patterns in the thousands of pages of court filings, emails, and other records.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Epstein was convicted in 2008 on state charges of soliciting a minor under a non-prosecution agreement that allowed him to plead guilty to reduced charges.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> He was arrested again in July 2019 on federal sex trafficking charges involving minors and died in his Manhattan jail cell the following month.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
