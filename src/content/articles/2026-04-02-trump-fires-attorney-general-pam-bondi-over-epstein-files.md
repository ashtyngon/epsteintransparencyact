President **Donald Trump** removed Attorney General **Pam Bondi** on Thursday, April 2, announcing on Truth Social that she would leave the Justice Department and that Deputy Attorney General **Todd Blanche** would serve as acting attorney general.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup><sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup> Trump's statement praised Bondi and offered no reason for the change, but people familiar with his thinking told multiple outlets he had grown frustrated on several fronts — among them the DOJ's handling of the release of documents related to **Jeffrey Epstein** and what he viewed as a lack of prosecutions of his political opponents.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

"Pam Bondi is a Great American Patriot and a loyal friend, who faithfully served as my Attorney General over the past year," Trump wrote. "We love Pam, and she will be transitioning to a much needed and important new job in the private sector, to be announced at a date in the near future, and our Deputy Attorney General, and a very talented and respected Legal Mind, Todd Blanche, will step in to serve as Acting Attorney General."<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Bondi had served as the nation's top law enforcement officer during the implementation of the Epstein Files Transparency Act, the landmark legislation Trump signed earlier this year requiring federal agencies to declassify and publish records tied to Epstein's activities and associates.

## Bipartisan Criticism Preceded Firing

Bondi drew fire from both sides of the aisle over the DOJ's approach to the file releases. Democrats accused her office of selectively redacting documents to shield politically connected individuals, while Republicans expressed frustration with the pace of compliance.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The tension escalated in early March when the Republican-led House Oversight Committee voted, 24-19, to subpoena Bondi for testimony about the DOJ's review process. Five Republicans — Nancy Mace, who put the motion forward, Tim Burchett, Lauren Boebert, Michael Cloud and Scott Perry — joined every Democrat, a rare cross-party rebuke of a sitting attorney general from the president's own party.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

People familiar with Trump's thinking told several outlets that the president had been frustrated with Bondi on multiple fronts, including the DOJ's handling of the Epstein releases and a perception that he was not fully committed to transparency on the matter, as well as a lack of successful prosecutions of his political opponents.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> Trump had repeatedly touted the transparency act as a signature achievement.

## Blanche Steps In as Acting AG

Deputy Attorney General **Todd Blanche**, who previously served as one of Trump's personal defense attorneys before joining the administration, will step in as acting attorney general, Trump said.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Reports indicated Trump was weighing a permanent replacement, with EPA Administrator Lee Zeldin described as a likely pick.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

Blanche was present alongside Bondi during the contentious closed-door briefing on March 18 when Democratic members of the House Oversight Committee walked out in protest. His elevation to the top DOJ post could reshape the department's approach to the remaining Epstein document releases.

The departure caps months in which the Epstein files dominated scrutiny of Bondi's tenure, though Trump himself did not cite the matter in announcing the change. Legal analysts noted that the move could signal a shift in the pace and scope of future releases.

The change at the top of the Justice Department came amid sustained pressure from lawmakers in both parties who have pressed for fuller disclosure of Epstein-related records and questioned the DOJ's review process.

The DOJ has published about 3 million pages under the transparency act — roughly half of the files it holds — with the remainder withheld for reasons that include protecting survivors' personal information and avoiding interference with active investigations.<sup>[4](#ref-4)</sup> Lawmakers from both parties have disputed whether the releases amount to full compliance.
