## Epstein Case Background

Jeffrey Epstein was convicted in 2008 on state charges of soliciting a minor in Florida. He served 13 months in county jail under a widely criticized plea agreement that allowed him work-release privileges.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

In July 2019, federal prosecutors in New York charged Epstein with sex trafficking of minors and conspiracy. He died by apparent suicide in his Metropolitan Correctional Center cell on August 10, 2019, while awaiting trial.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Ghislaine Maxwell, Epstein's longtime associate, was convicted in December 2021 on charges including sex trafficking of minors and conspiracy. She is serving a 20-year federal prison sentence. Maxwell recruited and groomed underage girls for Epstein to abuse over multiple years.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Congress passed the Epstein Transparency Act in December 2024, mandating declassification of federal records related to Epstein's crimes and associates.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The FBI began its review of Epstein-related records under the new law earlier this year.

Courts have released multiple batches of previously sealed materials from civil cases involving Epstein and Maxwell. These document releases have identified numerous individuals who had contact with Epstein, though appearing in released files does not indicate wrongdoing.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The appearance of survivors at Trump's address occurs as litigation and investigations related to Epstein's criminal network continue in multiple jurisdictions.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
