Physician and longevity expert Peter Attia has stepped aside from his role as a contributor at CBS News following the emergence of emails connecting him to Jeffrey Epstein, according to Reuters.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Attia had joined CBS News to provide medical and health expertise to the network.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The decision to step aside came after emails surfaced linking him to the late financier and convicted sex offender.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Details of the Departure

The specific nature and content of the emails were not detailed in Reuters' report.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The circumstances under which Attia may have communicated with Epstein, the timeline of any contact, and the context of their relationship remain unclear based on available reporting.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

CBS News had not issued a public statement regarding Attia's departure at the time of Reuters' reporting.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Attia is a physician who trained at Johns Hopkins Hospital and previously worked at McKinsey & Company before founding a medical practice focused on longevity science. He hosts the podcast "The Drive," which focuses on health, medicine, and metabolic science. His work has made him a recognized figure in health and wellness circles.

## Background on Document Releases

The Epstein Transparency Act, signed into law in 2024, mandated the declassification and public release of government records related to Epstein's case.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Federal agencies including the FBI began reviewing Epstein-related records under the law.
