Attorney General Pam Bondi has relocated to military housing following threats related to the Epstein case, according to the New York Daily News.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The move comes as Bondi oversees the Justice Department's handling of matters related to convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein. The Daily News reported that Bondi received threats connected to the Epstein case that led to the housing relocation.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Military housing for high-ranking government officials provides enhanced security measures not typically available at private residences. The arrangement places Bondi under the protection of military installations designed to secure senior government personnel facing credible threats.

## Bondi's Role in Epstein Case Oversight

As Attorney General, Bondi directs the Justice Department's implementation of the [Epstein Transparency Act](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law), which became law in January 2026. The statute requires federal agencies to declassify and release government records related to Epstein's activities and associates.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The law mandated establishment of a review board to oversee document releases, with limited exceptions for national security and ongoing investigations. Bondi's Justice Department controls the pace and scope of these releases.

Since the law's enactment, advocates and lawmakers have pressed the Justice Department to accelerate review and release of Epstein-related files. The [FBI has begun reviewing records](/news/fbi-begins-epstein-records-review) under the transparency requirements, though critics have challenged the speed and completeness of document releases.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

Bondi's confirmation as Attorney General in January 2026 placed her at the center of decisions regarding implementation of the Transparency Act and oversight of federal investigations into Epstein's network of associates.

## Florida Connection

Bondi previously served as Florida's Attorney General from 2011 to 2019, a period that overlapped with significant developments in the Epstein case. Epstein maintained a residence in Palm Beach, Florida, where he was first investigated and prosecuted on state charges of soliciting a minor in 2008. He was required to register as a sex offender following that conviction.

Epstein died by apparent suicide in August 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges in Manhattan. His longtime associate Ghislaine Maxwell was later convicted in December 2021 on federal charges of sex trafficking minors. Maxwell is currently serving a 20-year federal prison sentence.

The threats that prompted Bondi's move to military housing represent the latest security concern for officials connected to the Epstein case, which has drawn intense public attention since the Justice Department began releasing documents under the Transparency Act.
