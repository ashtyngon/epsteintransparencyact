A documentary exploring Jeffrey Epstein's criminal network is now available for streaming on Arte.tv, the Franco-German public broadcaster.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The film, titled "Epstein Files - Power, Money, Human Trafficking," is available on the network's digital platform.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Arte.tv is a European cultural television channel and digital platform jointly operated by French and German public broadcasters.

## Documentary Focus

The documentary's title indicates it examines three core elements of the Epstein case: the power structures that enabled his crimes, the financial networks he cultivated, and the human trafficking operation he directed.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Arte.tv made the full-length documentary available for public viewing through its streaming service.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The broadcaster has previously produced investigative documentaries on international criminal cases and matters of public interest.

The film's availability on a major European public broadcasting platform marks continued European media attention to the case. Epstein's activities extended to France, the United Kingdom, and other European locations, making the case a subject of international investigative reporting.

## The Epstein Case

Jeffrey Epstein was a financier who died in federal custody in August 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> He had been arrested in July 2019 on charges of sex trafficking of minors in Florida and New York.

Epstein was previously convicted in 2008 on state charges of soliciting a minor in Florida. That conviction resulted from a controversial plea agreement that allowed him to avoid federal prosecution at the time.

His death in a Manhattan jail cell was ruled a suicide by the New York City medical examiner. The circumstances generated widespread public scrutiny and multiple federal investigations into the Bureau of Prisons' handling of his detention.

## International Dimensions

Several investigations into associates and enablers of Epstein's trafficking operation have been launched by authorities in multiple countries. European authorities have examined Epstein's activities in France and the United Kingdom, where he maintained properties and business connections.

The Arte.tv documentary addresses these international dimensions of the case. Arte.tv reaches viewers across the European Union through its television channel and digital streaming service, providing French and German-speaking audiences access to investigative reporting on the case.

The release comes as public interest in documentary and investigative reporting on the case has increased following greater availability of court documents and records. The Epstein Transparency Act, enacted in the United States, requires federal agencies to review and declassify documents related to Epstein.

Arte.tv has not announced whether the documentary will be made available with subtitles in additional languages beyond French and German.
