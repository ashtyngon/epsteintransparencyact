The Justice Department's release of millions of files from its investigation into Jeffrey Epstein has triggered high-profile firings, resignations, and criminal investigations across multiple countries, NBC News reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The document release has affected officials and business leaders both in the United States and abroad, according to NBC News.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The network reported the files "have shaken the upper echelons of power across the globe."<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

NBC News reported that "a number of active criminal investigations overseas" have been launched following the file release.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The report did not specify which countries have opened probes or name individuals under investigation.

## High-Profile Departures

The files have resulted in both terminations and voluntary departures from positions of power, NBC News reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The network described these as "high-profile firings and resignations" in the U.S. and abroad but did not name specific individuals.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Multiple resignations following the Epstein files release have been documented in separate reporting. [Goldman Sachs' top lawyer resigned](/news/2026-02-13-goldman-sachs-top-lawyer-resigns-after-named-in-epstein-files) after being named in the files.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> [Dubai's ports chief stepped down](/news/2026-02-13-dubai-ports-chief-resigns-after-hundreds-of-secret-epstein-emails-exposed) after hundreds of emails were exposed.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup> [Hyatt billionaire Thomas Pritzker resigned](/news/2026-02-17-hyatt-billionaire-thomas-pritzker-resigns-over-epstein-ties) over documented ties to Epstein and Maxwell.<sup>[4](#ref-4)</sup>

## Document Release Background

The Justice Department's file release follows the enactment of the Epstein Transparency Act, which mandated the declassification and public release of government records related to Epstein's crimes and associates.<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup> The law was [signed into law](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law) in January 2026.<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup>

The newly released materials represent millions of files from the Justice Department's investigation into Epstein's sex trafficking operation, according to NBC News.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The [FBI began its review of Epstein-related records](/news/fbi-begins-epstein-records-review) under the new transparency law earlier this year.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup>

The volume of documents exceeds previous partial releases that emerged through civil litigation and criminal proceedings against Ghislaine Maxwell, who was convicted of sex trafficking in December 2021. Epstein died in federal custody in August 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges.

[France has launched two separate Epstein probes](/news/2026-02-18-french-prosecutors-launch-two-epstein-probes-call-for-victim-complaints), with prosecutors calling for victim complaints.<sup>[7](#ref-7)</sup> [UK police established a national task force](/news/2026-02-14-uk-police-launch-national-task-force-for-epstein-trafficking-allegations) to investigate trafficking allegations that emerged from the files.<sup>[8](#ref-8)</sup>

The international scope of the fallout reflects Epstein's network of associates across business, politics, and academia in multiple countries.
