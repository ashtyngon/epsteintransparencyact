First lady **Melania Trump** delivered a six-minute livestreamed statement on April 9 addressing the **Jeffrey Epstein** files, a move that caught White House aides off guard, CNN reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The statement, in which she called for public hearings on the Epstein matter, represented one of the most substantive public interventions by a first lady on an active legal and political controversy in recent memory. She did not take questions from reporters afterward.

## A First Lady Who 'Does Her Own Thing'

CNN reported that while the statement stunned senior White House staff, it was consistent with **Melania Trump**'s established pattern of operating independently from the West Wing's communications apparatus.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Multiple sources familiar with her thinking told CNN that the decision to address the Epstein files publicly was driven by the first lady's monthslong preoccupation with press coverage and internet speculation linking her and President **Donald Trump** to Epstein's social circle. Photographs of the Trumps with Epstein and **Ghislaine Maxwell** at social events in the late 1990s and early 2000s have circulated widely since the files began to be released.

Fortune reported that the statement appeared designed to get ahead of the narrative by positioning the first lady as an advocate for transparency rather than a subject of speculation.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

## Statement Called for Public Hearings

In her remarks, Melania Trump said the American public deserved full access to the Epstein-related documents and urged Congress to hold open hearings where survivors could share their experiences. She emphasized that she was speaking as a mother and as someone who believes in accountability.

The first lady did not directly address any specific allegations contained in the released files, nor did she comment on her husband's connections to Epstein. She focused instead on the broader principles of transparency and the rights of survivors to be heard.

KSAT reported that the statement was produced by the first lady's own communications team, separate from the White House press office, and that it was streamed simultaneously on multiple social media platforms.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

## Mixed Reception

The statement drew immediate reaction from across the political spectrum. Some commentators praised her for breaking with the typical restraint expected of first ladies on politically sensitive matters, while others questioned the timing and motivations behind the address.

Several survivors' advocacy groups noted that while they welcomed any public figure calling for hearings, they would reserve judgment until concrete action followed. The question of whether Congress would actually schedule the public hearings Melania Trump called for remained open as of April 10.

White House officials declined to comment on whether the president was consulted before or informed about the statement in advance. The president's social media accounts did not reference the first lady's remarks in the hours following the livestream.

The statement added another layer of complexity to an already volatile political environment surrounding the Epstein files, which have become one of the most divisive issues of the current administration.
