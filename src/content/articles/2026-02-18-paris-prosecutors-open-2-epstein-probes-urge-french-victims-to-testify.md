The Paris prosecutor's office has opened two criminal investigations related to Jeffrey Epstein and issued a public call for potential French victims to come forward with testimony.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The announcement represents the latest step in France's expanding scrutiny of Epstein-related allegations. In mid-February, [French authorities established a specialized task force](/news/2026-02-14-france-sets-up-special-team-to-review-epstein-files-for-french-nationals) to review recently released Epstein files for connections to French nationals.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The prosecutor's office did not disclose the specific nature of the charges under investigation or identify potential targets of the probes.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## French Victims Urged to Testify

French authorities issued a public appeal urging victims in France to provide testimony as part of the investigations.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The prosecutor's office has not specified what timeframe or conduct the investigations cover.

The Paris prosecutor's office is already [investigating a French diplomat](/news/2026-02-15-french-prosecutors-investigate-diplomat-in-epstein-probe) in a separate Epstein-related probe announced in mid-February.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

French modeling executive Jean-Luc Brunel, who worked with Epstein, was found dead in a Paris jail cell in February 2022 while awaiting trial on charges of rape of minors and human trafficking. Brunel had been arrested in December 2020 at Charles de Gaulle Airport as he attempted to board a flight to Senegal.

## International Investigation Expansion

The Paris investigations come as [multiple European nations have launched Epstein-related probes](/news/2026-02-18-epstein-files-revelations-spark-new-european-police-probes) following document releases in recent weeks.<sup>[4](#ref-4)</sup>

In mid-February, UK police confirmed they are [assessing trafficking claims](/news/2026-02-18-uk-police-assess-epstein-trafficking-claims-at-stansted-airport) related to alleged activity at Stansted Airport.<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup> British authorities also established a national task force to investigate Epstein trafficking allegations.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup>

In January, President Donald Trump signed the Epstein Transparency Act into law, mandating declassification and public release of government records related to Epstein's activities.<sup>[7](#ref-7)</sup> The FBI began reviewing records under the new law in February.<sup>[8](#ref-8)</sup>

Jeffrey Epstein died in a Manhattan jail cell in August 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges. His associate Ghislaine Maxwell was convicted in December 2021 on federal charges of sex trafficking and conspiracy and is serving a 20-year prison sentence.

Potential victims with information relevant to the French investigations have been directed to contact the Paris prosecutor's office.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> No timeline for completion of the probes has been announced.
