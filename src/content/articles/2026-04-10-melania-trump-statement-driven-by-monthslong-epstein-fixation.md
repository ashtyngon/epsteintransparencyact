First lady Melania Trump's April 9 public statement about **Jeffrey Epstein** caught White House aides off guard and was driven by what CNN described as her "monthslong fixation on press coverage and internet speculation" about Epstein ties, the network reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The unscheduled statement, delivered from the White House, was not coordinated with the president's communications team. Aides learned of the remarks only shortly before Melania Trump appeared before cameras, according to CNN's reporting.

## The Statement

Melania Trump read prepared remarks in which she directly addressed speculation linking her and her family to Epstein. Fortune reported that she declared: "The lies linking me with the disgraceful Jeffrey Epstein need to end today."<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

She did not take questions from reporters after delivering the statement. The remarks lasted approximately four minutes and covered her frustration with media coverage and online discussion of alleged Epstein connections to the Trump family.

The first lady did not reference specific allegations or name particular media outlets. She characterized the coverage broadly as unfounded and damaging to her family.

KSAT reported that the statement was notable for its direct and personal tone, which departed from the first lady's typically reserved approach to public communications.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

## Monthslong Buildup

CNN reported that Melania Trump had grown increasingly focused on media coverage linking the Trump family to Epstein in the months following the initial document releases in February.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The coverage included reporting on photographs of **Donald Trump** with Epstein at social events in the 1990s and early 2000s, as well as Epstein's membership at Trump's Mar-a-Lago club.

The president has said he had a falling out with Epstein years before the financier's 2019 arrest and has denied knowledge of Epstein's criminal activities. Those statements have not stopped persistent media scrutiny and social media speculation.

Sources told CNN that the first lady monitored coverage closely and discussed it with a small circle of personal advisers outside the White House communications apparatus.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## White House Reaction

The president's communications team scrambled to respond after the statement was delivered. Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt issued a brief comment saying the president supported the first lady's decision to address the matter publicly.

Behind the scenes, CNN reported that aides were concerned the statement would draw additional attention to the Epstein connection rather than quieting it.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Some advisers worried the remarks would be interpreted as an acknowledgment that the coverage had been effective in raising questions.

Other White House officials took a more positive view, arguing that the first lady's direct approach could help put the matter to rest with the president's base.

## Political Context

The statement came one day before House Oversight Chairman James Comer announced he would hold public hearings with Epstein victims. Melania Trump did not address congressional oversight of the Epstein files or DOJ document releases. Her remarks focused exclusively on personal allegations against her family.

**Ghislaine Maxwell**, Epstein's convicted associate, was not mentioned in the statement.

## Media Response

The statement generated extensive coverage. Analysts noted it was the first time a sitting first lady had directly addressed the Epstein matter in public remarks. Fortune noted that the first lady's language — referring to Epstein as "disgraceful" — represented one of the strongest condemnations from anyone in the Trump orbit.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The April 9 statement has not been followed by additional remarks from the first lady on the topic.
