Howard Lutnick, the Commerce Secretary, maintained a years-long business relationship with Jeffrey Epstein that included Epstein holding a financial stake in a Lutnick-founded company, according to newly released documents.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The files show Epstein held an ownership interest in BGC Partners, a brokerage firm Lutnick founded, Mother Jones reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The documents were released under the Epstein Transparency Act, which requires federal agencies to declassify records related to Epstein's activities and associations.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The disclosure provides new details about the financial ties between Lutnick and the convicted sex offender.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The exact terms of Epstein's investment and the dates of the business relationship were not specified in the reporting.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Lutnick's Financial Ties Under Scrutiny

Lutnick serves as Commerce Secretary in the Trump administration while remaining chairman and CEO of Cantor Fitzgerald, the financial services firm where he has worked since 1983.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> BGC Partners spun off from Cantor Fitzgerald as a separate interdealer brokerage handling trades between financial institutions.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The revelation comes amid [ongoing political fallout over Lutnick's Epstein connections](/news/2026-02-15-commerce-secretary-lutnick-s-epstein-ties-become-gop-liability), which became a liability for Republican lawmakers after the document releases.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Haverford College, where Lutnick is a major donor, has faced [pressure to remove his name from campus buildings](/news/2026-02-19-haverford-college-faces-calls-to-strip-lutnick-library-name-over-epstein-ties) due to the Epstein ties.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

BGC Partners operates in the institutional brokerage space, facilitating electronic and voice-based trading for banks, hedge funds, and other financial entities.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Epstein's stake in the firm represents another example of his investments in mainstream financial companies during his career.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
