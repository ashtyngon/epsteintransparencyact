A candidate for Texas comptroller purchased Jeffrey Epstein's former New Mexico ranch, drawing sharp concern from Republican officials in the state.

The candidate acquired the property known as Zorro Ranch, which was named in multiple lawsuits related to Epstein's sex trafficking operation, according to The Center Square.

The comptroller oversees Texas's finances and serves as the state's chief tax collector, revenue estimator, and treasurer.

## Property's Dark History

The ranch served as one of Epstein's primary residences. Court documents and victim testimony identified the New Mexico property as a location where women were trafficked.

After Epstein's death in federal custody, court-appointed representatives for his estate sold his properties to compensate victims through a restitution program.

## No Public Response

The candidate's campaign has not issued a statement about the property purchase. The Republican primary takes place in March 2026.

The purchase surfaces as [federal agencies continue releasing documents](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law) under the Epstein Transparency Act, with lawmakers pressing for fuller disclosure of names and connections in the files.
