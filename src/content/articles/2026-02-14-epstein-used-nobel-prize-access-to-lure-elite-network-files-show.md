Jeffrey Epstein referenced the head of the Nobel Peace Prize committee in invitations and messages to Richard Branson, Larry Summers, and Steve Bannon, according to newly released files reported by The Independent.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The documents show Epstein repeatedly referenced Thorbjorn Jagland -- former Norwegian prime minister (1996-1997), former secretary general of the Council of Europe (2009-2019), and chairman of the Norwegian Nobel Committee -- in communications with businessmen, academics, and political figures, according to The Independent.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The contacts occurred after Epstein's 2008 conviction in Florida for soliciting prostitution from a minor and continued until close to his death on August 10, 2019 at the Metropolitan Correctional Center.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Branson, Summers, Bannon Named in Communications

Branson, the Virgin Group founder, received invitations from Epstein referencing access to Jagland, according to the files.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Summers, who served as U.S. Treasury Secretary (1999-2001) and later as president of Harvard University (2001-2006), appeared in communications where Epstein referenced his Nobel connections.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Summers' Harvard tenure overlapped with a period when Epstein donated $9.1 million to the university between 1998 and 2008, according to previous reporting.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> Bannon, who later became chief strategist to President Trump in January 2017, was also contacted by Epstein referencing his association with the committee head, according to The Independent.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Gifts to Jagland

[Emails reported last week](/news/2026-02-13-nobel-chief-jagland-got-gifts-from-epstein-emails-reveal) showed Jagland received gifts from Epstein.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup> The new documents show Epstein referenced the Nobel committee leader's visits in outreach to other contacts.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The documents show a method across multiple contacts: Epstein would reference a connection with a prominent institutional figure in outreach to others, according to The Independent.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Broader Context

Epstein's Nobel-linked outreach was part of a broader pattern of contacts across business, government, and academia documented in the files. Recent releases -- part of the 3.5 million pages, 180,000 images, and 2,000 videos released under the Epstein Transparency Act signed November 19, 2025 -- have documented his [contacts with university leaders](/news/2026-02-13-college-presidents-professors-named-in-epstein-files-fallout),<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> [lawyers named in the files](/news/2026-02-14-epstein-emails-expose-ex-clifford-chance-lawyer-s-hidden-ties),<sup>[4](#ref-4)</sup> and business executives including Kathryn Ruemmler, former Obama White House counsel and Goldman Sachs general counsel.<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup>
