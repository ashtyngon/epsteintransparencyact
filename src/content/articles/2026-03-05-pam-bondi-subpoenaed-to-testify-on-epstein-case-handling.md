**Pam Bondi** has been subpoenaed to testify over her handling of the **Jeffrey Epstein case**, according to Sports Radio 93.3 KJR.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Bondi served as Florida Attorney General from 2011 to 2019.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> During that time, her office had jurisdiction over criminal matters in Florida, where Epstein maintained a Palm Beach residence and where allegations against him originated.

The subpoena relates to how Bondi's office handled the Epstein case during her tenure.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Florida authorities had investigated Epstein beginning in 2005 after Palm Beach police received reports involving underage girls at his mansion.

## Florida's Role in Epstein Prosecution

Epstein was convicted in 2008 on state charges of soliciting a minor following a controversial plea agreement. That agreement allowed him to plead guilty to lesser state charges rather than face federal prosecution on sex trafficking allegations. He served approximately 13 months in county jail with work-release privileges.

The 2008 plea deal drew intense criticism when details became public years later. State prosecutors in Florida accepted the agreement, which also included provisions preventing federal prosecution of Epstein and potential co-conspirators.

Bondi's tenure as Attorney General began in 2011, three years after Epstein's conviction. Questions about whether her office pursued additional investigations or charges during her eight years in office now form the basis of congressional scrutiny.

## Related Developments

Epstein was arrested again in July 2019 on federal sex trafficking charges in New York. He died in his Manhattan jail cell in August 2019. His associate **Ghislaine Maxwell** was convicted in December 2021 on federal charges of sex trafficking minors and is serving a 20-year prison sentence.

Bondi has remained a public figure since leaving the Florida Attorney General's office in 2019. She now serves as U.S. Attorney General after being confirmed in 2025. Her previous role overseeing state prosecutions in Florida during a critical period of Epstein scrutiny has drawn renewed attention following [recent congressional hearings on the Epstein files](/news/2026-02-13-bondi-grilled-on-epstein-files-why-ag-resists-full-document-release).

The Epstein Transparency Act, signed into law in 2025, has accelerated document releases and congressional inquiries into how officials handled allegations against Epstein over multiple decades.
