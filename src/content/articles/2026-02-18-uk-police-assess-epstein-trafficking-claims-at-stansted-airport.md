UK police are assessing allegations that Jeffrey Epstein trafficked women on flights through London Stansted Airport.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Essex Police, which has jurisdiction over the airport located northeast of London, confirmed it is reviewing the claims.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The assessment involves allegations that Epstein used flights through Stansted as part of trafficking operations.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

London Stansted Airport serves as a major hub for private aviation in the United Kingdom. The facility handles both commercial and private flights, making it a potential transit point for international travel between the United States and Europe.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## UK Authorities Face Renewed Scrutiny

The Metropolitan Police Service announced in August 2021 that it had completed a review of allegations against Epstein and determined no further action would be taken, stating that potential offenses would have occurred outside UK jurisdiction or would be time-barred.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The current assessment by Essex Police represents a separate inquiry focused specifically on alleged trafficking activities at Stansted Airport.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

British authorities previously launched a [national task force for Epstein trafficking allegations](/news/2026-02-14-uk-police-launch-national-task-force-for-epstein-trafficking-allegations) in February 2026 following the release of thousands of documents under the Epstein Transparency Act.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

His longtime associate Ghislaine Maxwell was convicted in December 2021 on federal charges of sex trafficking and conspiracy. She is currently serving a 20-year federal prison sentence.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
