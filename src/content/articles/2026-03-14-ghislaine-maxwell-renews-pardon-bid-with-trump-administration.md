**Ghislaine Maxwell** is continuing efforts to secure a presidential pardon from **President Donald Trump**, according to **La Voce di New York**.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Maxwell, the British socialite convicted in December 2021 of sex trafficking minors in connection with **Jeffrey Epstein**, is currently serving a 20-year federal prison sentence.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The report indicates she remains focused on obtaining clemency from the Trump administration.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Maxwell was found guilty on five of six counts related to recruiting and grooming underage girls for Epstein to abuse between 1994 and 2004.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> U.S. District Judge **Alison Nathan** sentenced her to 240 months in federal prison in June 2022.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Prosecutors had sought at least 30 years.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Maxwell's Failed Appeals

Maxwell has mounted multiple legal challenges to overturn her guilty verdict since her conviction. In December 2022, she appealed her conviction to the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit, arguing that prosecutorial errors and an improper jury instruction warranted a new trial.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> That appeal was denied in January 2024.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Maxwell subsequently filed a petition for a writ of certiorari with the U.S. Supreme Court in April 2024, asking the justices to review her case.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The Supreme Court declined to hear her appeal in June 2024, letting her conviction stand.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Her legal team also challenged her conditions of confinement and treatment while detained.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> In February 2025, Maxwell [offered to reveal information](/news/2026-02-15-ghislaine-maxwell-offers-to-reveal-truth-in-exchange-for-clemency) in exchange for clemency consideration.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Trial Evidence and Conviction

At trial, prosecutors presented evidence that Maxwell facilitated Epstein's abuse by identifying victims, grooming them, and participating in some of the abuse herself.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The trial lasted six weeks and featured testimony from four women who said Maxwell recruited them as teenagers.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Maxwell's relationship with Epstein spanned decades, beginning in the early 1990s when she was his girlfriend and later becoming his close associate and accused accomplice.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Epstein died by suicide in August 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges in a Manhattan jail cell.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Maxwell has been incarcerated at the Federal Correctional Institution in Tallahassee, Florida, since July 2022.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Her projected release date, with good time credit, is July 2037.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Pardon Process

Presidential pardon applications are typically submitted through the Office of the Pardon Attorney at the Department of Justice.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Presidents retain the constitutional authority to grant clemency without following that formal process.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Trump administration has issued several controversial pardons and commutations during both his first and current terms in office.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
