The Democratic National Committee and Democrats on the House Oversight Committee publicly questioned on April 3 whether President **Donald Trump** fired Attorney General **Pam Bondi** to obstruct the ongoing investigation into **Jeffrey Epstein**'s associates and the release of related documents.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The DNC released a statement calling the timing of Bondi's dismissal "deeply suspicious" and demanding answers from the White House about the president's motivations.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Timing Draws Scrutiny

Rep. **Robert Garcia** (D-CA), the ranking Democrat on the House Oversight Committee, highlighted that Bondi's firing came just days before she was scheduled to give a deposition before the committee regarding the DOJ's handling of Epstein file releases.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

"The attorney general was fired days before she was set to answer questions under oath about what happened to the Epstein files," Garcia said in a statement. "The American people deserve to know why."<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

Garcia noted that the committee had been seeking Bondi's testimony for weeks after reports emerged that DOJ officials had removed or reclassified certain Epstein-related documents before their scheduled release.

## Democrats Call for Investigation

House Oversight Democrats sent a letter to acting Attorney General Todd Blanche requesting that he preserve all communications related to Bondi's firing, including any discussions between the White House and DOJ leadership about Epstein file releases.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The letter also requested that Blanche commit to honoring Bondi's previously scheduled deposition, either by appearing himself or by producing a senior DOJ official with knowledge of the document release process.

Senate Democrats echoed the call. Sen. Sheldon Whitehouse (D-RI), a member of the Judiciary Committee, said the firing "raises the specter of obstruction" and called for the committee to hold hearings on the matter.

"When the president fires the attorney general days before she is supposed to testify about his connection to a sex trafficker, that is not a coincidence," Whitehouse said. "That is a pattern."<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Republican Response

Republican leaders dismissed the Democratic accusations as politically motivated. House Oversight Chairman James Comer (R-KY) said the president had "every right" to remove the attorney general and that Democrats were "manufacturing a scandal."

White House spokesperson said the firing was a personnel decision unrelated to any specific investigation and that the president maintained confidence in the DOJ's handling of its obligations under the Epstein Files Transparency Act.

Acting AG Blanche, in a separate Fox News appearance, said the suggestion that Bondi was fired over Epstein files was "simply not true."

## Legal Analysis

Legal experts said that while a president has broad authority to remove cabinet officials, the timing of Bondi's firing could become relevant if investigators later determined that the intent was to obstruct a specific proceeding.

Constitutional law scholars noted that the deposition Bondi was scheduled to give was a congressional proceeding, and that firing an official to prevent testimony before Congress could, under certain circumstances, constitute obstruction.

However, experts cautioned that proving such intent would be extremely difficult without direct evidence linking the firing to the deposition.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
