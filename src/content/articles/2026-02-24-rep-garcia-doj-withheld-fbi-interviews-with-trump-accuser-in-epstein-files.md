Rep. Robert Garcia (D-Calif.) said the Department of Justice may not have fully complied with the Epstein Transparency Act by withholding FBI interviews with a woman who has accused President Donald Trump of sexual assault.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Garcia's statement focuses on interviews the FBI conducted with the Trump accuser, which the congressman indicated should have been included in records released under the new transparency law.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The Epstein Transparency Act mandates disclosure of records related to Jeffrey Epstein's sex trafficking operation.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Department of Justice has not publicly addressed Garcia's claim about the withheld interviews.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Transparency Act Requirements

The Epstein Transparency Act requires federal agencies to declassify and release documents related to Epstein's crimes, associates, and connections to powerful figures.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The FBI began releasing records under the law earlier this year, though Garcia's statement suggests the scope of the initial release may be incomplete.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Garcia, who represents California's 42nd congressional district, sits on committees with oversight authority over the Justice Department.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> His public statement indicates congressional Democrats intend to scrutinize whether agencies are meeting their legal obligations under the statute.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The law passed both chambers of Congress with overwhelming bipartisan support before being signed into law in January 2025.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The extent of Epstein's connections to powerful figures in politics, business, and entertainment has remained a subject of intense public interest since his arrest in July 2019.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Garcia's statement indicates that questions about the scope of government records related to those connections continue as agencies work to comply with disclosure requirements.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Epstein Case Background
