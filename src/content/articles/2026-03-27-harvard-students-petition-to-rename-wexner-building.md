Students and faculty at Harvard University's Kennedy School of Government submitted a formal request to rename the Leslie H. Wexner Building and the Wexner-Sunshine Lobby, citing the designation of **Les Wexner** as a **Jeffrey Epstein** co-conspirator in a 2019 FBI document released under the Epstein Files Transparency Act.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The petition, filed with the university administration in late March, represents the most concrete step yet by the Harvard community to distance the institution from a donor whose ties to Epstein have been extensively documented in released files, WBUR reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Wexner, the billionaire founder of L Brands — the parent company of Victoria's Secret and Bath & Body Works — donated millions of dollars to Harvard over several decades. The Wexner Building at the Kennedy School has borne his name since its construction, funded in part by his philanthropy.

## The Co-Conspirator Designation

The FBI document identifying Wexner as a co-conspirator was produced during the bureau's investigation of Epstein and was among the files released to the public under the transparency act. The designation does not carry criminal charges but indicates that federal investigators identified Wexner as having played a role in the activities under investigation.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Wexner has publicly stated that he was deceived by Epstein, who served as his personal financial manager for more than a decade. He has said he severed the relationship in 2007, before Epstein's 2008 conviction. However, the co-conspirator designation in the FBI document suggests investigators viewed Wexner's involvement as more significant than a passive business relationship.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

Fortune reported that the renaming petition cited not only the FBI designation but also Wexner's role in providing Epstein with the New York townhouse that became central to Epstein's trafficking operation.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> Wexner transferred the property to Epstein under terms that have never been fully explained publicly.

## The Harvard Petition

The formal request was organized by a coalition of Kennedy School students and faculty members who argued that retaining the Wexner name on campus buildings was incompatible with the university's stated values and its obligations to survivors of sexual violence.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The petitioners asked the university to convene a formal review process, including input from the broader Harvard community, and to establish a timeline for a decision on the renaming. They also requested that the university make public any internal assessments it has conducted regarding its historical relationship with Wexner and Epstein.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Harvard has not publicly committed to a specific timeline for reviewing the request. The university's policies on building names require a formal process that can involve the Board of Overseers and other governance bodies.

## Ohio State Parallel

The Harvard petition emerged as Ohio State University simultaneously began its own review of multiple Wexner-named facilities. Ohio State President **Ravi Bellamkonda** is overseeing a five-step review process that applies to the Les Wexner Football Complex, the Wexner Medical Center, and other campus buildings bearing the donor's name.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

WOSU reported that the Ohio State process includes gathering input from university stakeholders, a committee assessment, and a final recommendation to the university's Board of Trustees.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup> The review was initiated after sustained pressure from students, faculty, and alumni following the release of the FBI document identifying Wexner as a co-conspirator.

Sports Illustrated reported that the Les Wexner Football Complex, one of the premier college football training facilities in the country, has become a particular focus of the debate, given the visibility of Ohio State's athletic program and the national attention surrounding the Epstein files.<sup>[4](#ref-4)</sup>

## Institutional Implications

The parallel reviews at Harvard and Ohio State reflect a broader reckoning within higher education over the legacy of donor relationships with individuals connected to Epstein. Universities across the country are grappling with questions about naming rights, donor vetting, and institutional accountability.

Legal experts have noted that renaming buildings can involve complex contractual issues, as donation agreements sometimes include perpetual naming provisions. Whether the FBI's co-conspirator designation provides sufficient grounds to invoke moral turpitude clauses — if such clauses exist in the relevant agreements — remains an open legal question.

Wexner has not been criminally charged in connection with Epstein's activities. His representatives have not publicly responded to the Harvard or Ohio State renaming efforts.
