The disclosure comes as Musk has made public statements about the Epstein case in recent months.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The billionaire entrepreneur, who leads Tesla and SpaceX, has called for greater transparency regarding those connected to the convicted sex offender on social media platforms.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Epstein's Criminal History

Epstein was arrested in July 2019 on federal sex trafficking charges.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Federal prosecutors accused him of operating a sex trafficking ring that recruited and abused dozens of underage girls at his residences in Manhattan and Palm Beach, Florida.

He died in his cell at the Metropolitan Correctional Center in New York on August 10, 2019, while awaiting trial.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The New York City medical examiner ruled his death a suicide by hanging.

The 2019 arrest came more than a decade after Epstein's initial conviction. In 2008, he pleaded guilty to state charges of soliciting a minor in Florida.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> He served 13 months in a county jail under a work-release program that allowed him to leave custody for up to 12 hours a day, six days a week.

That 2008 plea deal was negotiated with federal prosecutors in the Southern District of Florida. The arrangement faced intense criticism because it allowed Epstein to avoid federal charges and did not require him to cooperate with authorities in identifying possible co-conspirators.
