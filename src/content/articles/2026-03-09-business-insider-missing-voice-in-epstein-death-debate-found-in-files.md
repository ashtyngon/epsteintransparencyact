A witness account from someone housed near **Jeffrey Epstein** at the **Metropolitan Correctional Center (MCC)** when he died has been found in recently released government documents, according to **Business Insider**.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The document provides a perspective that has been largely absent from public debate about the circumstances of Epstein's death on **August 10, 2019**, Business Insider reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The account comes from an individual who was in proximity to Epstein at the federal detention facility in New York at the time.

## Context of Epstein's Death

Epstein died at MCC while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges involving dozens of underage girls.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> He had been arrested in July 2019 and denied bail.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The New York City medical examiner ruled his death a suicide by hanging.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> However, the circumstances surrounding his death—including security failures at the facility—have fueled widespread speculation and conspiracy theories.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

Epstein had been placed on suicide watch following an incident in his cell on **July 23, 2019**, but was removed from that status days before his death.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> He was supposed to have a cellmate and be checked on by guards every 30 minutes, but neither protocol was followed the night he died.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

## Security Failures and Criminal Charges

Two correctional officers responsible for monitoring Epstein were later charged with falsifying prison records to make it appear they had conducted required wellness checks when they had not.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> Both officers pleaded guilty to conspiracy charges and admitted to browsing the internet and sleeping instead of performing their duties.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

Security cameras that should have captured footage outside Epstein's cell malfunctioned, adding to questions about what occurred that night.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The Federal Bureau of Prisons faced intense scrutiny over the failures at MCC. The facility was chronically understaffed, and its infrastructure—including security camera systems—was outdated and poorly maintained.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

## Document Release Under Transparency Act

The witness account is part of tens of thousands of pages of Epstein-related materials being made public under the Epstein Transparency Act.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup> The law requires federal agencies to declassify and release records related to Epstein's case, his associates, and investigations into his crimes.

The **Department of Justice** has been releasing documents in phases, with [nearly 50,000 additional files expected](/news/2026-03-05-doj-to-release-nearly-50-000-more-epstein-files) in coming weeks.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup> The releases have included FBI investigation records, correspondence with federal prosecutors, and witness statements collected during probes into Epstein's sex trafficking operation.

Business Insider noted that the witness account from inside MCC provides testimony about conditions at the facility during Epstein's incarceration and death.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
