A New York newspaper has criticized the National Football League and the New York Giants for failing to act on emails between Giants co-owner Steve Tisch and convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Troy Record published an opinion piece February 24 with the headline "NFL's and Giants' inaction on Steve Tisch-Jeffrey Epstein emails is shameful."<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The editorial targets both the league and the team for their response to the correspondence, though full details of the emails' content and timing were not included in the available source material.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## No Public Statements From NFL or Giants

Neither the NFL nor the Giants organization have issued public statements addressing the emails referenced in the Troy Record opinion piece, according to available reporting.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Tisch serves as chairman and executive vice president of the Giants.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The team is one of the NFL's founding franchises and plays home games at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The NFL has constitutional authority to investigate and sanction team owners for conduct detrimental to the league.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> League governance documents grant the commissioner power to impose fines and other penalties on owners whose actions damage the NFL's reputation or violate league policy.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Epstein Files Under Congressional Scrutiny

Epstein died in federal custody in August 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> He was convicted in 2008 in Florida on state charges of soliciting a minor.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Documents related to Epstein continue to draw scrutiny from lawmakers and the public following passage of the [Epstein Transparency Act](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law), which requires federal agencies to declassify and release records related to the case.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Multiple members of Congress from both parties have pressed for greater disclosure regarding Epstein's connections to prominent figures in business, politics, and entertainment.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Federal agencies including the FBI and Department of Justice are conducting reviews to determine what classified materials can be released.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
