An opinion piece in **The Hill** has called for the immediate release of millions of documents related to **Jeffrey Epstein** that remain withheld by the **U.S. Department of Justice**, warning that the agency's handling of the files is damaging public trust in the federal government.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The opinion piece compared the situation to the **Pentagon Papers** scandal, which caused lasting damage to Americans' confidence in government during the Vietnam War era.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The author termed Epstein's files the "Pedophile Papers" and said the millions of documents being kept from Congress and the American people are eroding trust in federal institutions.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Pentagon Papers — leaked in 1971 — revealed the Department of Defense had systematically misled the public about the Vietnam War for years. The comparison suggests the current withholding of Epstein files represents a similar breach of public trust.

## Documents Released in Batches

The Justice Department has been releasing Epstein-related files following passage of the **Epstein Transparency Act**, which was signed into law in late December with overwhelming bipartisan support.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> The legislation requires federal agencies to declassify and release documents related to Epstein's criminal activities and associates.

In early March, DOJ [released nearly 50,000 files](/news/2026-03-05-doj-to-release-nearly-50-000-more-epstein-files) and announced plans to make additional documents public.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup> The **FBI began its formal review** of Epstein-related records under the new law earlier this year.<sup>[4](#ref-4)</sup>

Despite these releases, transparency advocates and some lawmakers have argued the pace remains too slow and that millions of documents continue to be withheld from public view.

**Attorney General Pam Bondi** was [subpoenaed to testify](/news/2026-03-05-pam-bondi-subpoenaed-to-testify-on-epstein-case-handling) before Congress in early March about DOJ's handling of the case.<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup> A **USA Today** opinion piece published in March called on Congress to act over what it characterized as DOJ obstruction of the files' release.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup>

Epstein was arrested in July 2019 on federal sex trafficking charges and died in his Manhattan jail cell one month later. His associate **Ghislaine Maxwell** was convicted in 2021 of sex trafficking minors and is serving a 20-year federal prison sentence. Epstein had previously been convicted in 2008 on state charges of soliciting a minor in Florida.
