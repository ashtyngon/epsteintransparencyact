The New Mexico Department of Justice announced it will reopen its criminal investigation into Jeffrey Epstein's Zorro Ranch, examining possible sex trafficking at the property.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The announcement came less than two weeks after The Guardian reported that federal agents did not appear to have ever searched Zorro Ranch.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The decision follows a public pressure campaign calling for a fuller accounting of the role the location played in the late financier's sex-trafficking conspiracy.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Guardian Report Prompted Action

The Guardian's February report revealed an apparent gap in federal investigation of the New Mexico property.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The revelation that federal agents had not searched the ranch prompted calls from the public for state authorities to take action.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The reopened investigation will focus on possible sex trafficking that may have occurred at Zorro Ranch.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The property was one of several locations associated with Epstein's criminal network.

Earlier this month, [New Mexico Attorney General Raúl Torrez announced a separate criminal investigation](/news/2026-02-19-new-mexico-ag-opens-criminal-probe-into-bodies-buried-at-epstein-ranch) into reports of bodies buried at the ranch, following claims in newly released documents.

The New Mexico Department of Justice did not specify a timeline for the reopened investigation or detail what evidence investigators would examine.
