Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor's Thursday arrest marks him as the highest-profile figure to face criminal consequences for ties to convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein, according to Axios.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Supporters of the late Virginia Giuffre see the arrest as a long-coming victory for the woman who once sued Mountbatten-Windsor for sexual abuse.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Police arrested Mountbatten-Windsor on suspicion of misconduct in public office, authorities said.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> In a statement about the arrest, police cited emails allegedly showing that the former prince may have forwarded confidential trade documents to Epstein.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The emails were made public in the latest Justice Department document release.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The newly released batch of Epstein files also include photographs showing Mountbatten-Windsor kneeling over a woman lying on the floor, Axios reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Andrew's Denials

Mountbatten-Windsor has consistently denied wrongdoing in connection with his relationship with Epstein, according to Axios.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Giuffre previously filed a civil lawsuit against Mountbatten-Windsor alleging sexual abuse.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> She was among the most prominent alleged victims to publicly identify Epstein and individuals in his social circle.

The arrest follows years of scrutiny over [Mountbatten-Windsor's ties to Epstein](/news/2026-02-19-prince-andrew-arrested-in-london-over-epstein-documents), including questions about meetings and communications between the two men. [Police have expanded their investigation](/news/2026-02-22-police-widen-prince-andrew-investigation-after-epstein-files-arrest) after the latest document release showed the extent of communication between Mountbatten-Windsor and Epstein.

## Wider Investigation

Metropolitan Police have [conducted searches at Royal Lodge](/news/2026-02-22-police-continue-search-at-prince-andrew-s-royal-lodge-over-epstein-ties), Mountbatten-Windsor's residence, as part of the expanded investigation. [Former Prime Minister Gordon Brown has called for a probe](/news/2026-02-22-gordon-brown-demands-probe-into-andrew-s-raf-base-epstein-meetings) into whether Epstein held meetings at Royal Air Force bases.

The U.K. government is [considering removing Mountbatten-Windsor from the line of royal succession](/news/2026-02-21-u-k-considers-removing-andrew-from-royal-succession-after-arrest) following the arrest.

Epstein died in federal custody in August 2019 while awaiting trial on sex trafficking charges. His associate Ghislaine Maxwell was convicted in December 2021 on charges including sex trafficking of minors.
