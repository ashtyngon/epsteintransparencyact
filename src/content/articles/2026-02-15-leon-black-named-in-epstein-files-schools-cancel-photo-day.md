Schools canceled yearbook photo sessions with a photography company after its founder, billionaire Leon Black, was named in recently released Epstein files, according to Daily Kos.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Black is the founder of Apollo Global Management. Schools terminated contracts with his photography business following the document release, though the specific institutions have not been publicly identified.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Educational Institutions Respond

Universities have faced scrutiny over Epstein connections, with DOJ files showing Epstein had contacts at the University of Alabama.<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup> Multiple college presidents and professors were named in earlier document releases.
