The Justice Department's handling of the Jeffrey Epstein files has devolved into a full-blown political crisis this week, fracturing the conservative coalition and triggering accusations that Attorney General **Pam Bondi** is surveilling members of Congress who dare to search the unredacted documents for specific names.

House Judiciary Committee Democrats are now demanding that DOJ allow them and certain staffers to review the files at the Capitol complex itself, rather than at an agency facility where their searches have been systematically logged. The revelation that the Justice Department tracked which names lawmakers searched for emerged during Bondi's combative **five-hour testimony** on Wednesday, igniting a firestorm that has united both progressive Democrats and the MAGA base against the attorney general.

It's a stunning reversal for an administration that campaigned on transparency. Instead of sunlight, Congress got surveillance.

## The Surveillance Apparatus Revealed

The mechanics of DOJ's monitoring operation came to light during what sources describe as a "bombshell" moment in Bondi's House Judiciary Committee hearing. Lawmakers discovered that when they traveled to Justice Department facilities to review unredacted Epstein files, the agency wasn't just granting access—it was building a database of exactly which names each member of Congress was investigating.

According to CBS News, multiple lawmakers are now accusing Bondi of outright "spying" on their search history during Epstein file viewings. The practice effectively creates a chilling effect on congressional oversight, turning what should be transparent cooperation into an intelligence-gathering operation against the legislative branch.

House Judiciary Democrats' response was swift and unequivocal: bring the files to us, or face a constitutional showdown over separation of powers. Their demand to review documents at the Capitol complex, rather than DOJ-controlled facilities, represents an institutional rebuke to an attorney general who has already drawn fire for [resisting full document release](/articles/2026-02-13-bondi-grilled-on-epstein-files-why-ag-resists-full-document-release).

The surveillance revelation adds teeth to earlier concerns that Bondi's DOJ was [tracking lawmaker searches](/articles/2026-02-13-doj-tracking-lawmaker-epstein-file-searches-bondi-document-reveals) through internal documentation systems.

Bondi's Wednesday testimony revealed more than just the surveillance apparatus. It exposed a Justice Department leadership that survivors say has shown them contempt.
