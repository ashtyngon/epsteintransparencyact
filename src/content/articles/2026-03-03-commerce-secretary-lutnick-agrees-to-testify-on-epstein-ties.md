Commerce Secretary **Howard Lutnick** voluntarily agreed to testify before the House Oversight Committee about his relationship with convicted sex offender **Jeffrey Epstein**, CNBC reported on March 3, as discrepancies emerged between his public statements and documentary evidence about when their association ended.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Lutnick, the CEO of Cantor Fitzgerald before joining the Trump administration, has previously stated that he cut off contact with Epstein in 2005. However, records that surfaced in recent document releases showed that Lutnick and members of his family had lunch with Epstein in 2012 — seven years after the claimed cutoff and four years after Epstein's sex offense conviction in Florida.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The interview before the committee is scheduled for May 6, according to reporting by Axios.<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup>

## Discrepancies in the Timeline

The Washington Post first reported the 2012 lunch, citing records that placed Lutnick and his family at a meal with Epstein well after the period Lutnick had publicly described as the end of their relationship.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> The discrepancy raised questions about the accuracy of other statements Lutnick has made regarding the nature and duration of his Epstein ties.

NBC News reported that Lutnick also acknowledged visiting Epstein's private island in the U.S. Virgin Islands, though he has not specified when those visits occurred or provided details about their purpose.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

The combination of the 2012 lunch record and the acknowledged island visit has created a timeline that does not align with Lutnick's public assertion of a clean break in 2005. Committee Chairman **James Comer** (R-KY) said the testimony would address these and other questions about Lutnick's relationship with Epstein.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Voluntary Appearance

Lutnick's decision to appear voluntarily distinguishes his situation from that of other Epstein associates who have resisted congressional inquiry. The House Oversight Committee has issued subpoenas to several individuals named in the files, and Attorney General Pam Bondi faces a separate subpoena for sworn testimony on the DOJ's handling of document releases.

CNBC reported that Lutnick characterized his agreement to testify as a demonstration of transparency, though his representatives did not address the specific discrepancy between his claimed 2005 cutoff and the 2012 lunch.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Bloomberg reported that Lutnick's testimony could address questions about financial transactions between Cantor Fitzgerald and Epstein-related entities, an area that has drawn interest from committee investigators.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup> Lutnick has not been accused of any criminal wrongdoing in connection with Epstein.

## Political Context

The testimony is politically significant because Lutnick is a sitting Cabinet secretary in the Trump administration. His appearance before a Republican-led committee on a matter involving Epstein underscores the bipartisan nature of the congressional investigation, which has pursued associates across party lines.

Axios reported that the May 6 date was negotiated between Lutnick's legal team and the committee to allow adequate preparation time.<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup> The interview is expected to be conducted in a closed-door setting initially, with the possibility of a public hearing depending on the substance of Lutnick's statements.

Other current and former government officials named in the Epstein files have faced varying degrees of congressional scrutiny. The committee has prioritized individuals who hold or recently held positions of public trust, on the grounds that their relationships with Epstein are of heightened public interest.

## Cantor Fitzgerald Connection

Lutnick led Cantor Fitzgerald through the September 11 attacks, which killed 658 of the firm's employees. His leadership during that crisis elevated his public profile and made him a prominent figure in the financial industry long before his appointment to the Commerce Department.

The question of whether Cantor Fitzgerald had any business dealings with Epstein or his financial entities has not been fully addressed in public reporting. Committee investigators have indicated they intend to explore the professional as well as personal dimensions of the Lutnick-Epstein relationship during the May testimony.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
