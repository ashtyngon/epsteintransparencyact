CNN reported Thursday that Jeffrey Epstein's former Zorro Ranch in New Mexico is facing renewed scrutiny, though the outlet did not provide details about the nature or scope of the investigation.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The 10,000-acre property in Stanley, New Mexico, has been the subject of law enforcement attention for years. The FBI conducted searches of the ranch in recent days, according to earlier reporting.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> Court documents and victim testimony have identified the ranch as one of several locations where Epstein allegedly committed sexual abuse involving minors.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Property History and Federal Searches

Epstein purchased the remote New Mexico property in the 1990s. The ranch includes a main residence, an airplane hangar, and other structures spread across the high desert landscape near Santa Fe.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The property remained under his control until his death in August 2019.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

In 2019, following Epstein's arrest on federal sex trafficking charges, the FBI raided the ranch as part of its investigation. Federal agents removed evidence from the property during that search, though the full scope of materials seized has not been made public.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The FBI's recent return to the property marks a development in ongoing investigations related to Epstein's activities. Federal investigators [searched the ranch](/news/2026-03-10-fbi-searches-epstein-s-zorro-ranch-after-new-revelations) following what sources described as new revelations, though the specific nature of those revelations has not been disclosed publicly.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

## Sale and Transfer of Property

The ranch was sold in 2020 to an Oklahoma ministry for $18 million. At the time of Epstein's death, the property was valued at approximately $27.5 million, meaning the sale price was significantly below its assessed value.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The property transfer came amid ongoing legal disputes over Epstein's estate and compensation for his victims. The estate has faced multiple civil lawsuits from women who say Epstein sexually abused them at various properties he owned or controlled.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Epstein's New Mexico Ties

Epstein maintained connections to New Mexico for decades. Following his 2008 conviction on state charges of soliciting a minor in Florida, he registered as a sex offender in New Mexico, where he continued to own the ranch.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The 2008 conviction came under a controversial non-prosecution agreement that allowed Epstein to plead guilty to lesser state charges. He served 13 months in a work-release program that permitted him to leave custody six days per week.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

New Mexico state officials have faced criticism for their handling of Epstein-related matters. Questions have been raised about oversight of the property and Epstein's compliance with sex offender registration requirements during the years he owned the ranch.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Ongoing Investigations

Epstein was arrested again in July 2019 on federal sex trafficking charges involving dozens of underage girls. He died in his Manhattan jail cell on August 10, 2019, in what was ruled a suicide by the New York City medical examiner.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The renewed scrutiny of the Zorro Ranch property represents one of several active federal inquiries related to Epstein's criminal enterprise and the network of associates who allegedly enabled his crimes. Court documents unsealed in recent months have revealed additional details about Epstein's operations and the individuals involved.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
