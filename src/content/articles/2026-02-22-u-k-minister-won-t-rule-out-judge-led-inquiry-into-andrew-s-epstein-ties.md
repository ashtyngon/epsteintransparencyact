Police continued searching Prince Andrew's former residence at Royal Lodge on the Windsor estate Sunday, while a U.K. government minister said a judge-led inquiry into Andrew's ties to convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein has not been ruled out, according to The Guardian.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Bridget Phillipson, the education secretary, said the government would not rule out such an inquiry but characterized the discussion as premature while police continue their investigation, The Guardian reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Phillipson was representing the government when she made the statement.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The searches at Royal Lodge continued Sunday at Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor's former home on the Windsor estate, according to The Guardian.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Calls for Removal From Succession

Calls have mounted for Mountbatten-Windsor to be removed from the royal line of succession, The Guardian reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The U.K. government was previously reported to be [considering legislation to remove Andrew from the line of succession](/news/2026-02-21-u-k-considers-removing-andrew-from-royal-succession-after-arrest) following his arrest.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Police [widened their investigation into Andrew after his arrest](/news/2026-02-22-police-widen-prince-andrew-investigation-after-epstein-files-arrest) in connection with the Epstein case.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
