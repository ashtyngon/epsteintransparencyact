The co-executors of Jeffrey Epstein's estate have agreed to settle a class action lawsuit filed by survivors of Epstein's abuse for at least $25 million, according to court documents.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The settlement involves Darren Indyke, Epstein's longtime attorney, and Richard Kahn, his accountant.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Both men served as co-executors of the estate following Epstein's death in August 2019.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The class action lawsuit was filed against the co-executors by survivors of Epstein's abuse.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The $25 million figure represents the minimum settlement amount.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Indyke and Kahn were appointed to administer Epstein's estate after his death.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The settlement represents one of multiple legal actions stemming from Epstein's abuse of victims over multiple decades.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Epstein's former associate Ghislaine Maxwell was convicted in 2021 on federal charges of sex trafficking and conspiracy for her role in recruiting and grooming minor victims.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> She is currently serving a 20-year prison sentence.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The settlement with the estate co-executors continues the legal aftermath of Epstein's crimes as survivors pursue compensation through civil litigation.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
