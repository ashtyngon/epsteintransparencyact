**Olly Robbins**, the former permanent secretary at the UK Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office, testified to the House of Commons Foreign Affairs Select Committee on April 21, 2026 that his department was placed under "serious pressure" to push through **Peter Mandelson**'s appointment as ambassador to Washington despite security vetting concerns.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The hearing followed Prime Minister **Keir Starmer**'s statement to the Commons the previous day, in which he said the Foreign Office had made a "deliberate decision" to withhold information about the failed vetting from MPs and from him personally.

## Robbins' Testimony

According to coverage of the proceedings, Robbins told the committee that political pressure played a role in how the Foreign Office handled Mandelson's vetting. The committee, chaired by Labour MP **Emily Thornberry**, was tasked with examining how a security vetting recommendation against Mandelson was overruled and how the appointment proceeded despite those concerns.

The session marked the first formal parliamentary inquiry into the institutional failures that led to Mandelson — a senior Labour figure with documented ties to **Jeffrey Epstein** — being placed in the most prestigious role in the British diplomatic service.

## How the Mandelson Appointment Unraveled

The full scope of Mandelson's relationship with Epstein has been pieced together through documents released by the U.S. Department of Justice and through reporting by ITV News, the Washington Post, CNN, and others.

Mandelson was appointed UK ambassador to Washington in December 2024 and took up the role in February 2025. He was fired by Starmer in September 2025 after newly released DOJ documents showed his contacts with Epstein continued long after Epstein's 2008 conviction.

In February 2026, UK police arrested Mandelson on suspicion of misconduct in public office in connection with passing sensitive government information to Epstein. He has not been charged.

The vetting failure became public on April 16, 2026, when reports emerged that Mandelson had failed his security vetting before the appointment but that Foreign Office officials had cleared him anyway.

## Political Stakes for Starmer

The Robbins testimony is among the most consequential moments in the political crisis the Mandelson scandal has created for Starmer's government. The prime minister has staked his defense on the argument that he was misled by the Foreign Office bureaucracy, rather than that he made a flawed political judgment in appointing a longtime ally to the role.

Robbins' testimony — that the Foreign Office was under "serious pressure" — could either support or undercut that defense, depending on which political quarter Robbins identifies as the source of the pressure. Labour MPs, opposition parties, and the press have closely scrutinized the proceedings.

Conservative leader **Kemi Badenoch** and Liberal Democrat leader **Sir Ed Davey** have both accused Starmer of misleading Parliament. Two of Starmer's most senior aides, chief of staff **Morgan McSweeney** and communications director **Tim Allan**, resigned earlier in 2026 over the handling of the appointment.
