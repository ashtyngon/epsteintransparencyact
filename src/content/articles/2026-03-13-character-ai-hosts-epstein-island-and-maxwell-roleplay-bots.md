A controversial artificial intelligence chatbot platform is hosting user-created bots that roleplay scenarios involving Jeffrey Epstein's private island and impersonate Ghislaine Maxwell, according to Futurism.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Character.AI, a platform that allows users to create and interact with AI-powered chatbots, is currently hosting multiple bots related to Epstein and his convicted associate Maxwell, Futurism reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The bots enable users to engage in fictional scenarios connected to Epstein's island, where numerous sexual assaults of minors occurred.

Users have created bots designed to simulate interactions with Maxwell and scenarios set on Epstein's island, according to the report.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Maxwell Serving 20 Years for Sex Trafficking

Maxwell was convicted in December 2021 of sex trafficking and conspiracy charges for her role in recruiting and grooming underage girls for Epstein to abuse between 1994 and 2004.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> A federal jury in Manhattan found her guilty on five of six counts after a monthlong trial.

She is currently serving a 20-year federal prison sentence at FCI Tallahassee, a low-security federal prison in Florida.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Maxwell was sentenced in June 2022 by U.S. District Judge Alison Nathan.

Epstein died by suicide in a Manhattan jail cell in August 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> His death occurred at the Metropolitan Correctional Center, where he was being held without bail.

## Epstein's Island Was Site of Crimes

Epstein's private island in the U.S. Virgin Islands, known as Little St. James, was the site of numerous alleged sexual assaults against minors over a period of years.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Court documents and victim testimony identified the island as a primary location where Epstein and Maxwell trafficked and abused underage girls.

The 70-acre island was purchased by Epstein in 1998 for approximately $8 million. Multiple victims testified during Maxwell's trial about being taken to the island and assaulted there.

Character.AI is an artificial intelligence company that provides chatbot technology allowing users to create conversational agents with distinct personalities.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The platform enables users to design bots that can engage in extended conversations and roleplay scenarios.

The company has not publicly commented on its policies regarding bots that simulate real-world criminals or crime scenarios, according to Futurism.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Federal Transparency Law in Effect

The [Epstein Transparency Act](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law), which was signed into law in January 2025, mandates the declassification and public release of federal documents related to Epstein's crimes and his associates.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The FBI [began its review of Epstein-related records](/news/fbi-begins-epstein-records-review) under the new law earlier this year.

The law requires federal agencies to review and release documents within specific timeframes, with limited exceptions for ongoing investigations and national security concerns.
