Princess Eugenie has lost her role as Royal Patron of the Anti-Slavery Collective, the anti-trafficking organization she co-founded, following renewed scrutiny of her family's connections to convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein, according to Amanda Matta's Substack.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The timing of the patronage change follows the release of previously withheld Epstein-related documents that have brought fresh attention to her uncle, Prince Andrew, and his relationship with Epstein.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Anti-Slavery Collective has not issued a public statement explaining the decision or announcing a replacement patron. The organization's website and social media channels have removed references to Eugenie's role.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Andrew's Epstein Ties

Prince Andrew's friendship with Epstein has been documented in court filings and media reports. Andrew stepped back from royal duties in 2019 following a BBC interview about his association with Epstein.

Virginia Giuffre, one of Epstein's accusers, alleged in court documents that she was trafficked to Prince Andrew when she was 17. Andrew consistently denied the allegations. In 2022, Andrew settled a civil lawsuit with Giuffre without admitting liability.

The Department of Justice [released nearly 50,000 previously withheld documents](/news/2026-03-05-doj-to-release-nearly-50-000-more-epstein-files) earlier this month under the [Epstein Transparency Act](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law), including materials related to the FBI's investigation.

## Ongoing Scrutiny

The loss of royal patronage marks a significant shift for the Anti-Slavery Collective, which has used Eugenie's profile to raise awareness of human trafficking issues. Royal patronages typically continue unless a patron resigns or the organization requests a change.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Eugenie, who is not a working member of the royal family, maintains a private career and selectively supports charitable causes.

Multiple investigations continue into Epstein's network and the handling of his case by law enforcement. Congressional committees have [subpoenaed multiple figures](/news/2026-03-04-house-panel-subpoenas-bill-gates-kathryn-ruemmler-in-epstein-probe) connected to Epstein's circle for testimony.

Epstein died in federal custody in 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges. His associate Ghislaine Maxwell was convicted in 2021 of sex trafficking and conspiracy charges related to recruiting and grooming underage girls for Epstein to abuse. She is currently serving a 20-year federal prison sentence.

[Prince Andrew was arrested in London](/news/2026-02-19-prince-andrew-arrested-in-london-over-epstein-documents) in February 2026 on charges related to the Epstein case. [UK police have expanded their investigation](/news/2026-02-20-scotland-yard-expands-andrew-probe-questions-protection-officers-on-epstein-link) to question his former protection officers about his Epstein connections.
