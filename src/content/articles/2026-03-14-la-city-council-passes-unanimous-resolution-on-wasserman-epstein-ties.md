The Los Angeles City Council voted 12-0 on March 14 to approve a resolution urging the LA28 Olympic organizing committee's board of directors to investigate the connections between its chairman, **Casey Wasserman**, and the **Jeffrey Epstein** files.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The unanimous vote came after the resolution was rescheduled twice in previous weeks, reflecting the political sensitivity surrounding the 2028 Olympic Games and Wasserman's prominent role in the city's preparations to host the event.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## LA28 Defies Council

Despite the council's unanimous rebuke, LA28's Executive Committee issued a statement defiantly backing Wasserman as chairman of the organizing committee.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> The committee said it had reviewed the available information and maintained full confidence in Wasserman's leadership of the 2028 Games.

The defiant response from LA28 set up a standoff between the city's elected government and the private organizing body responsible for delivering the Olympics. The council resolution is non-binding, meaning it carries symbolic and political weight but cannot compel LA28 to take specific action.

Wasserman's name appeared in documents released under the Epstein Files Transparency Act in connection with **Ghislaine Maxwell**, Epstein's longtime associate who was convicted in 2021 of sex trafficking charges. The nature of Wasserman's connection to Epstein and Maxwell has been a subject of public debate since the documents surfaced.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Mayor Bass Had Called for Resignation

Mayor **Karen Bass** had previously called for Wasserman to step down from his role leading the Olympic organizing committee, citing the need to protect the integrity of the 2028 Games.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup> Bass said the Epstein-related revelations had created an unacceptable distraction from the city's Olympic preparations.

The council resolution echoed Bass's concerns, stating that public confidence in the Games requires transparency and accountability from its leadership. Council members who spoke in favor of the resolution emphasized that they were not prejudging Wasserman's conduct but were calling for a thorough and independent investigation.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

"The people of Los Angeles deserve to know that the person leading our Olympics has been fully vetted," one council member said during the debate, according to ABC7.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Olympic Preparations Continue

The political controversy has not yet disrupted the operational preparations for the 2028 Games, which are scheduled to begin in July 2028. LA28 has said its planning remains on track and that venue construction, transportation planning, and security preparations are proceeding on schedule.

However, the International Olympic Committee has been monitoring the situation. The IOC has not publicly commented on the Wasserman controversy but has historically intervened in organizing committee leadership disputes when they threaten to undermine the credibility of the Games.

Wasserman has denied any wrongdoing and has said his appearance in the Epstein files does not reflect any improper conduct. His representatives have argued that the documents do not establish any participation in or knowledge of Epstein's criminal activities.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

Deadline reported that several LA28 sponsors have privately expressed concern about the controversy's potential impact on the Games' commercial program, though none have publicly withdrawn their support.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>
