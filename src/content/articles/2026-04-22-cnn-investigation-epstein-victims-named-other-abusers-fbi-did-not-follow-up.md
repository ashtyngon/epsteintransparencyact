A CNN investigation published April 22, 2026 found that **more than a dozen Epstein victims told the FBI** during interviews that **Jeffrey Epstein** or **Ghislaine Maxwell** facilitated sexual encounters with other powerful men, but the released files show little evidence federal agents followed up on those leads.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The CNN review of FBI interview memos identified victim allegations against more than a half-dozen named men, including Wall Street executives, a former U.S. senator, a wealthy psychiatrist, and a Hollywood film producer. The names of accused men were redacted in many memos, and the documents do not include corroborating investigative work or details of how agents followed up.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Specific Allegations Against Named Men

CNN identified specific allegations against two prominent figures in the documents:

**Jes Staley**, the former CEO of Barclays and a longtime JPMorgan executive, was named by a victim who said Epstein directed her to massage Staley and that he then sexually assaulted her, according to the CNN report.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

**Leon Black**, the billionaire co-founder and former CEO of Apollo Global Management, was named by multiple victims. One woman said Black became "sexual" during a massage that Epstein arranged, and that she fled the room. Another woman alleged that Black raped her in a separate incident years earlier.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Both Staley and Black have previously denied wrongdoing in connection with Epstein. Staley was sued by JPMorgan in 2023 over his role in maintaining the bank's relationship with Epstein. Black left Apollo in 2021 after an outside review found he paid Epstein $158 million for tax and estate planning work, with no allegations of wrongdoing made by the law firm conducting that review at the time. Sen. Ron Wyden (D-Ore.) is currently investigating an additional $12 million in payments from Black to Epstein.

## FBI's Stated Position

**FBI Director Kash Patel** has publicly stated there is "no credible information" that Epstein trafficked his victims to other men, according to CNN. The agency's position contrasts with the volume of victim accounts the network reviewed in the released files.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

To date, only Epstein and Maxwell have been criminally charged in the United States with sex trafficking related to the case. Epstein died in federal custody in August 2019 while awaiting trial. Maxwell was convicted in December 2021 on five federal counts and is serving a 20-year sentence.

## What the Files Don't Show

The CNN investigation highlights what is missing from the released files as much as what is in them. The interview memos reviewed by the network typically:

- Redacted the names of accused men
- Did not include evidence of investigative steps taken to corroborate the accounts
- Did not document whether prosecutors evaluated the allegations for charging decisions
- Did not include records of any interviews with the men named by victims

That gap has fueled bipartisan demands in Congress for the Justice Department to release the underlying investigative materials, including any FBI 302 reports, agent notes, and interview transcripts that go beyond the redacted summaries published so far.

## Context

The investigation comes the day before the DOJ Office of the Inspector General announced its own probe into how the department has complied with the Epstein Files Transparency Act. Lawmakers from both parties have accused the DOJ of using heavy redactions to protect politically connected figures named in Epstein files.
