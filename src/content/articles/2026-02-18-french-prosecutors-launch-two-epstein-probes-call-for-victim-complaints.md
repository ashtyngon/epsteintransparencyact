Paris prosecutors have opened two separate criminal investigations linked to Jeffrey Epstein and are calling on victims to come forward with testimony, according to a Courthouse News report.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Prosecutor Laurence Beccuau called on victims who may have never spoken up before to file formal complaints or make witness accounts to feed French and foreign investigations.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The prosecutor's announcement explicitly references coordination with foreign investigations, according to the report.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Beccuau's specific appeal for victims who have not previously come forward indicates French authorities are seeking additional witnesses beyond those who have already provided testimony in other proceedings.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The call for witness testimony comes as [multiple countries have launched investigations](/news/2026-02-18-epstein-files-revelations-spark-new-european-police-probes) following document releases related to Epstein's network. French authorities [previously established a specialized team](/news/2026-02-14-france-sets-up-special-team-to-review-epstein-files-for-french-nationals) to review Epstein files for French nationals, and Paris police [conducted a raid at the Arab World Institute](/news/2026-02-16-paris-police-raid-arab-world-institute-in-epstein-investigation) as part of their investigation.
