The head of Dubai's DP World ports operation has departed after newly released documents showed he was mentioned more than **4,700 times** in the Epstein files, according to CBS News.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

**Sultan Ahmed bin Sulayem's** departure from **DP World** -- which operates ports across six continents and employs approximately 103,000 people -- followed the release of documents showing correspondence with the convicted sex offender, according to BBC News.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The company issued a statement citing "strategic realignment."<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The content of the thousands of documented mentions of bin Sulayem in the Epstein files has not been publicly disclosed.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Other Executive Departures

Bin Sulayem joins a list of figures who have departed their positions after being named in the Epstein files. [Goldman Sachs' top lawyer resigned](/news/2026-02-13-goldman-sachs-top-lawyer-resigns-after-named-in-epstein-files) after emails surfaced in the files.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

In each case, an executive was named in documents related to Epstein, followed by that person's departure. DP World has not commented on the nature of the correspondence between bin Sulayem and Epstein.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Continued Redactions

Rep. Ro Khanna, after [another figure named in the Epstein files stepped down](/news/2026-02-13-khanna-demands-names-after-major-epstein-associate-steps-down), called for full disclosure of all names in the files.<sup>[4](#ref-4)</sup>

Attorney General Pam Bondi has not agreed to demands for full document release. Members of Congress have publicly named individuals they say are being protected by redactions.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The FBI has stated that redactions protect ongoing investigations and privacy.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Outstanding Questions

The nature and substance of bin Sulayem's mentions in the Epstein files remain unknown to the public. Large portions of the documents remain redacted.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Congress has moved to depose Les Wexner, described in the files as Epstein's closest known financial associate. Whether lawmakers will seek testimony from international figures like bin Sulayem has not been announced.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
