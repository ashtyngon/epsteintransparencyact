A West Palm Beach concierge physician maintained a professional relationship with Jeffrey Epstein during the final years of his life, according to newly released court documents and financial records reviewed by the **Miami New Times**.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

**Dr. Steven A. Bensusan** provided medical services to Epstein from approximately 2016 until the financier's death in 2019, records show.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The physician operated a concierge medical practice in West Palm Beach, offering round-the-clock access to patients who paid annual retainer fees.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Medical Services and Financial Arrangements

Financial records obtained by the Miami New Times indicate that Epstein paid Bensusan's practice substantial retainer fees for on-call medical services.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Epstein received immediate medical attention at his Palm Beach estate without visiting traditional medical facilities.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Bensusan made house calls to Epstein's waterfront mansion on multiple occasions, according to visitor logs and witness statements cited in the report.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The physician's services included routine health maintenance and emergency medical consultations.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Court documents show that Bensusan's name appears in financial records seized from Epstein's properties during federal investigations.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The records detail payments made through Epstein's corporate entities to the physician's medical practice.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Bensusan operated his concierge medical practice in the West Palm Beach area for more than a decade, serving affluent clients in the region.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> His practice model emphasized 24-hour availability and personalized care for patients willing to pay premium fees for such access.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The physician maintained an active medical license in Florida throughout the period he treated Epstein, according to state licensing records referenced in the Miami New Times report.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Document Discovery

Attorneys representing Epstein victims have sought information about individuals who provided services to Epstein during the years he continued his abuse, according to court filings cited in the report.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The financial records help establish the network of professionals who worked for Epstein despite his 2008 conviction on state charges of soliciting a minor.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
