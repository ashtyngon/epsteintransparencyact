Students at **Bard College** in Annandale-on-Hudson, New York, have launched a campaign called "Take Back Bard" demanding accountability from President **Leon Botstein** over his connections to convicted sex offender **Jeffrey Epstein**, according to Inside Higher Ed.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The student movement comes as the college's Faculty Senate has separately urged the Board of Trustees to develop a leadership transition plan for Botstein, who has led the liberal arts institution since 1975.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Epstein Connections Revealed

Documents released under the Epstein Files Transparency Act and investigative reporting have revealed that Botstein traveled to Epstein's private island in the U.S. Virgin Islands in 2012, three years after Epstein completed his sentence for a 2008 conviction for soliciting a minor in Florida.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

In 2013, Botstein wrote communications expressing admiration for Epstein, according to records reviewed by Inside Higher Ed.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The nature and extent of the relationship between the college president and the convicted sex offender have become a focal point of both the student campaign and the faculty's push for a leadership change.

The Board of Trustees has hired the law firm **WilmerHale** to conduct an independent investigation into Botstein's relationship with Epstein.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> WilmerHale, a prominent Washington-based firm with experience conducting internal investigations for major institutions, is expected to examine the full scope of contacts between Botstein and Epstein, including any financial transactions or institutional connections.

## Student Demands

The Take Back Bard campaign has organized demonstrations on campus and circulated an open letter calling on the Board of Trustees to act on the Faculty Senate's recommendation. Students said they are seeking transparency about the full extent of Botstein's ties to Epstein and accountability for any actions that may have compromised the college's integrity.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

"We deserve to know whether our president's relationship with a convicted sex trafficker influenced any decisions at this institution," the student letter stated, according to Inside Higher Ed.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Students have specifically asked whether Epstein or entities connected to him made donations to Bard College, whether Epstein was involved in any recruitment or programming decisions, and whether Botstein introduced any students or faculty to Epstein or his associates.

## Faculty Senate Action

The Faculty Senate's recommendation for a leadership transition plan stopped short of calling for Botstein's immediate resignation but signaled a loss of confidence in his continued leadership.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Faculty members said the Epstein revelations have created a distraction that is damaging the college's reputation and its ability to attract students and donors.

Botstein has served as Bard's president for more than 50 years, making him one of the longest-serving college presidents in the United States. He has overseen significant expansion of the institution, including the establishment of international programs and partnerships.

Botstein has not publicly commented on the Take Back Bard campaign or the Faculty Senate recommendation. The Board of Trustees said in a statement that it takes the matter seriously and is awaiting the results of the WilmerHale investigation before making any decisions about leadership.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
