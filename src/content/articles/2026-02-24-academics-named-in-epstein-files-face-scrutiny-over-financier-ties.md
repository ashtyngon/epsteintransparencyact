Multiple academics appear in documents related to convicted sex offender **Jeffrey Epstein**, according to a report by **Times Higher Education**.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The outlet examined how the financier cultivated relationships with researchers and scientists, with one source describing him as "surrounded by smart people."<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The report comes as institutions continue reviewing their connections to Epstein, who [maintained extensive ties to academic communities](/news/2026-02-13-college-presidents-professors-named-in-epstein-files-fallout) before his August 2019 death in federal custody while awaiting trial on sex trafficking charges.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Universities Reckon With Epstein Ties

Several universities have faced scrutiny over donations received from Epstein and relationships between faculty members and the financier. MIT, Harvard, and other institutions have conducted internal reviews and implemented new policies regarding donor relationships and conflicts of interest.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Epstein was convicted in 2008 of state charges of soliciting a minor under a controversial plea agreement. He was arrested again in July 2019 on federal sex trafficking charges involving dozens of underage girls.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Document Releases Name Network Members

Courts have released multiple batches of previously sealed documents related to civil litigation against Epstein associate **Ghislaine Maxwell**, who was convicted in December 2021 of sex trafficking and related charges for facilitating Epstein's abuse of minor girls.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The records include deposition testimony, flight logs, and correspondence detailing Epstein's network of contacts across academia, business, and politics.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Federal authorities signed the **Epstein Transparency Act** into law, requiring release of previously classified documents related to Epstein and his associates. The FBI has begun reviewing records under the legislation, which passed with bipartisan support in Congress.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Epstein engaged with scientists and academics in fields including evolutionary biology, neuroscience, and theoretical physics, funding scientific initiatives through his foundation while [using philanthropy to establish credibility within academic communities](/news/2026-02-20-why-academics-populate-epstein-files-status-hunger-not-just-money).<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
