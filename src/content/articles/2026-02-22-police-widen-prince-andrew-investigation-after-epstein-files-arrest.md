U.K. police have expanded their investigation into former Prince Andrew following his arrest in connection with the Epstein case, CBS News reported Saturday.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The investigation is "widening," according to CBS News correspondent Elizabeth Palmer, who reported the arrest was "still making waves in the U.K. on Saturday."<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

CBS News did not specify which law enforcement agency is conducting the investigation, what additional investigative steps are being taken, or what charges Andrew may face.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Andrew's Arrest

[Andrew was arrested earlier this week in London over his handling of Epstein-related documents](/news/2026-02-19-prince-andrew-arrested-in-london-over-epstein-documents).<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> The arrest led to [the British government considering legislation to remove Andrew from the line of royal succession](/news/2026-02-21-u-k-considers-removing-andrew-from-royal-succession-after-arrest).<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

[Scotland Yard had previously expanded its probe to question Andrew's protection officers about his links to Epstein](/news/2026-02-20-scotland-yard-expands-andrew-probe-questions-protection-officers-on-epstein-link).<sup>[4](#ref-4)</sup>

## Royal Family Response

[King Charles has publicly distanced himself from his brother over the Epstein connections](/news/2026-02-21-king-charles-distances-himself-from-prince-andrew-over-epstein-ties).<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup> Andrew no longer performs official royal duties and ceased using the style "His Royal Highness" in official capacities after settling a 2022 civil lawsuit filed by Virginia Giuffre, who alleged she was trafficked to Andrew when she was 17.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup> Andrew denied the allegations and the settlement included no admission of liability.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup>

Andrew remains eighth in line to the British throne despite having no role in official royal functions.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup>

Jeffrey Epstein died in federal custody in August 2019 while awaiting trial on sex trafficking charges.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup> His associate Ghislaine Maxwell is serving a 20-year federal prison sentence for sex trafficking minors.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup>
