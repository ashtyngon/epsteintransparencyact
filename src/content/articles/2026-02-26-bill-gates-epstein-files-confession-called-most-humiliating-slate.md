**Bill Gates** acknowledged he hoped **Jeffrey Epstein** could help him secure a **Nobel Peace Prize**, according to Slate.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The admission came during an interview with ABC News, in which Gates discussed his past relationship with the convicted sex offender, Slate reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Gates said he believed Epstein's connections could assist him in winning the prestigious award, according to the report.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Slate characterized the revelation as potentially "the most humiliating" detail to emerge from the Epstein files, while noting it is "hardly the worst thing" contained in the broader document release.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Gates Met With Epstein After Conviction

Gates has previously acknowledged meeting with Epstein multiple times after Epstein's 2008 conviction on state charges of soliciting a minor.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The Microsoft co-founder has faced ongoing scrutiny over the nature and extent of his interactions with Epstein.

The Nobel Peace Prize is awarded annually by the Norwegian Nobel Committee in Oslo to individuals or organizations that have made significant contributions to peace. Past recipients include Martin Luther King Jr., Nelson Mandela, and Mother Teresa. The prize includes a medal, diploma, and monetary award.

Gates' former wife, **Melinda French Gates**, has previously stated that her discomfort with her then-husband's relationship with Epstein was a factor in their 2021 divorce.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The couple announced their separation after 27 years of marriage, with the Epstein connection emerging as one of several reported points of tension.
