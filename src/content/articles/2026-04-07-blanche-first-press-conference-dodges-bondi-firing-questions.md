Acting Attorney General **Todd Blanche** held his first press conference since assuming the role, declining to answer questions about the firing of **Pam Bondi** and contradicting his own earlier public statements about **Jeffrey Epstein** files, NBC News reported.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Blanche stood at the DOJ podium and took questions from assembled reporters for approximately 25 minutes. The session quickly turned contentious when journalists pressed him on two topics he appeared unwilling to address.

## Bondi Departure

When asked directly why Bondi was removed as attorney general, Blanche said personnel matters were "not something I'm going to discuss." He redirected to his priorities for the department, citing ongoing law enforcement operations and immigration enforcement.

Pressed again by a second reporter, Blanche repeated that he would not comment on the circumstances of Bondi's departure. "The president makes personnel decisions. That's his prerogative," Blanche said.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

**President Donald Trump** has not publicly explained his decision to remove Bondi. White House officials have offered varying accounts, with some citing policy disagreements and others describing a desire for a "fresh start" at the department.

Bondi herself has not made public statements since her removal. Her office at DOJ was cleared within 24 hours of the announcement, according to department staff.

## Epstein File Contradiction

The press conference took a more combative turn when reporters raised Blanche's recent statement that Epstein files "should not be a part of anything going forward."

Blanche denied making the statement, telling reporters he had "never suggested that the Department of Justice should move past these files."<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Multiple news outlets, including The Hill and the Washington Examiner, had reported his earlier remarks days prior.

Raw Story noted that Blanche became visibly uncomfortable when confronted with the contradiction, shifting his weight and pausing before answering follow-up questions.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> A reporter read back his earlier quote, and Blanche responded that his words had been "taken out of context" without elaborating on what context was missing.

NBC News reported that the exchange lasted several minutes, with Blanche repeatedly attempting to redirect to other topics before the questioning moved on.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Defense of DOJ Loyalty

Throughout the press conference, Blanche emphasized the department's alignment with the president's agenda. He described DOJ employees as "dedicated public servants working to carry out the president's vision for law enforcement."

Blanche stated that his tenure, whether temporary or permanent, would focus on ensuring the department operates as the president directs. He cited border enforcement, prosecution of violent crime, and combating drug trafficking as priorities.

The loyalty language echoed themes from Blanche's earlier career as a defense attorney representing Trump in federal cases in New York. Legal analysts noted that the explicit framing of DOJ as operating under presidential direction departed from traditional rhetoric about prosecutorial independence.

## Congressional Reaction

Members of the House Oversight Committee responded to the press conference with criticism. Democratic members said Blanche's denial of his earlier Epstein comments demonstrated he was "not credible" on the transparency issue.

Republican members who co-authored the Epstein Transparency Act did not immediately comment on the press conference. A spokesperson for Rep. Thomas Massie (R-KY) said the congressman was "reviewing the acting attorney general's remarks."

The committee has a pending subpoena for Bondi's testimony related to DOJ handling of Epstein files. Blanche's appointment has not changed the committee's plans to pursue that testimony, according to committee staff.
