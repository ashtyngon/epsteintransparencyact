**Bill Clinton** released a video statement Saturday after testifying under oath in the ongoing congressional investigation into **Jeffrey Epstein**, stating he "saw nothing" and "did nothing wrong" during his interactions with the late financier.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The former president also addressed questions about **Donald Trump's** connections to Epstein during his sworn testimony, according to reports.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Clinton's statement that he observed no wrongdoing by Trump in connection with Epstein has drawn skepticism from some observers.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Clinton's video statement marked his first public comments following the deposition, which was part of the congressional probe examining Epstein's network of associates and enablers.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

## Testimony Details

The deposition took place as part of an investigation authorized under the **Epstein Transparency Act**, which mandates the release of government records related to the case.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> Clinton had previously been [scheduled for deposition](/news/2026-02-27-bill-clinton-scheduled-for-deposition-in-epstein-probe) in the congressional inquiry.

Clinton's ties to Epstein have been documented through flight logs showing the former president traveled on Epstein's private aircraft multiple times in the early 2000s.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup> The nature and extent of those interactions have been subjects of congressional scrutiny.

The former president's testimony addressed questions about what he witnessed during his association with Epstein and whether he observed any criminal conduct.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

## Public Response

Clinton's assertion that he saw no wrongdoing has met with skepticism, according to reports.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The video statement represents Clinton's first direct public address of the testimony's contents.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

The congressional investigation continues to examine records and testimony from multiple individuals connected to Epstein's activities. The probe follows years of public scrutiny over how Epstein maintained relationships with prominent figures despite his 2008 conviction on state charges of soliciting a minor.
