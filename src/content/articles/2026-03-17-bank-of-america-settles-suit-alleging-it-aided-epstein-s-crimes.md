**Bank of America** has reached a proposed settlement in a lawsuit alleging the bank facilitated Jeffrey Epstein's sex trafficking operations, according to Rural Radio Network.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The settlement is non-binding, meaning it has not yet received final approval from the court or all parties involved.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Terms of the proposed agreement were not disclosed.

The lawsuit alleged that Bank of America aided Epstein's crimes by providing banking services that enabled his trafficking network to operate.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> A federal court [allowed the Bank of America lawsuit to proceed](/news/2026-02-13-court-allows-bofa-epstein-trafficking-lawsuit-to-proceed) in February 2026, rejecting the bank's motion to dismiss.

## Other Banks Settled Epstein Litigation

**JPMorgan Chase** and **Deutsche Bank** previously faced similar litigation over their banking relationships with Epstein. JPMorgan reached a $290 million settlement with Epstein victims in June 2023, while Deutsche Bank agreed to a $75 million settlement in July 2023. Both banks were accused of ignoring red flags about Epstein's activities while profiting from their relationship with him.

Epstein maintained accounts at multiple major financial institutions during the period when he was trafficking underage girls. Financial institutions' role in enabling Epstein's operations has become a focus of civil litigation by victims. Banks are alleged to have processed payments to victims and ignored suspicious transactions that should have triggered anti-money laundering reviews.

Federal judges must approve class action settlements to ensure they are fair and adequate for victims. The non-binding status of the Bank of America agreement means final terms may still change before court approval.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Epstein died in federal custody in August 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges. He had been convicted in 2008 on state charges of soliciting a minor in Florida, receiving a controversial plea deal that allowed him to serve just 13 months in a county jail with work release privileges.

The proposed Bank of America settlement comes as federal agencies continue implementing the [Epstein Transparency Act](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law), which requires the FBI and other government entities to declassify and release documents related to Epstein's activities and associates. The [FBI began its review of Epstein-related records](/news/fbi-begins-epstein-records-review) following the law's enactment earlier this year.
