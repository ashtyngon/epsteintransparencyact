No new criminal charges have emerged in the United States following the Justice Department's latest release of Epstein-related documents, according to CBS News.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Questions remain about whether the files will lead to additional prosecutions.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The report from CBS News correspondent Jake Rosen comes as news broke of [Prince Andrew's arrest in the United Kingdom](/news/2026-02-19-prince-andrew-arrested-on-document-sharing-charges-in-epstein-case) on charges related to the Epstein case.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Justice Department released its latest batch of Epstein files under the Epstein Transparency Act, which President Trump [signed into law](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law) earlier this year.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The [FBI began its review of Epstein-related records](/news/fbi-begins-epstein-records-review) following the law's implementation.<sup>[2](#ref-2)</sup>

## Prince Andrew Arrest in UK

Prince Andrew's arrest marks a significant development in the international scope of investigations stemming from the Epstein files.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> British authorities took action while U.S. prosecutors continue reviewing newly released documents.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The former prince has faced years of scrutiny over his relationship with Jeffrey Epstein. [His trade role details](/news/2026-02-19-prince-andrew-trade-role-details-emerge-in-epstein-investigation) have emerged as part of the ongoing investigation.<sup>[3](#ref-3)</sup>

[King Charles responded to his brother's arrest](/news/2026-02-19-king-charles-responds-to-prince-andrew-s-epstein-related-arrest) as the legal proceedings unfolded in the United Kingdom.<sup>[4](#ref-4)</sup>

## Document Release Background

The Epstein Transparency Act required declassification and release of government records related to Epstein's activities and associates.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Multiple document releases have occurred through both court proceedings and legislative action.<sup>[5](#ref-5)</sup>

Previous releases have identified numerous individuals who appeared in flight logs, visitor records, and civil depositions. Attorney General Pam Bondi [listed 300 names in the Epstein files release](/news/2026-02-15-ag-bondi-lists-300-names-in-epstein-files-release) during congressional testimony last week.<sup>[6](#ref-6)</sup>

Members of Congress have pushed for fuller disclosure. Rep. Nancy Mace [accused the DOJ of hiding names](/news/2026-02-15-rep-mace-accuses-doj-of-hiding-names-in-epstein-files-release) in the released files, while Rep. Thomas Massie [ripped the Trump DOJ over file redactions](/news/2026-02-15-massie-rips-trump-doj-over-epstein-file-redactions).<sup>[7](#ref-7)</sup>

The absence of new charges in the United States occurs as federal prosecutors continue evaluating materials from the document release.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The Justice Department has not announced a timeline for completing its review.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
