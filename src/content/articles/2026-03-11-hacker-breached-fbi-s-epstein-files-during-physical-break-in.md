A hacker accessed FBI files related to Jeffrey Epstein during a physical break-in at the bureau, The Independent reported Wednesday.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The incident involved an intruder gaining physical access to FBI facilities and then compromising computer systems containing Epstein-related records, according to unnamed sources cited by the outlet.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Independent did not specify when the breach occurred, which FBI location was targeted, or what specific files were compromised.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The report also did not indicate whether any information was removed from FBI systems or distributed.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The FBI has not publicly confirmed the breach.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The bureau has not released details about the scope of the intrusion or whether the compromised files included witness statements, investigative materials, or information about individuals connected to Epstein's activities.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Transparency Act Review Underway

The breach comes as federal agencies work to implement the Epstein Transparency Act, [which became law earlier this year](/news/epstein-transparency-act-signed-into-law).<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The legislation requires government agencies to review and declassify records related to Epstein and his associates.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The FBI [began its formal review process](/news/fbi-begins-epstein-records-review) in response to the legislation.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The bureau has not publicly detailed how many Epstein-related records it possesses or provided a timeline for public release under the Transparency Act.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Transparency Act passed both chambers of Congress with bipartisan support.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> Multiple federal agencies are conducting reviews of their Epstein files under the new law, including the State Department and Treasury Department.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The FBI maintains extensive files on Epstein's activities and associates from multiple investigations spanning years.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The bureau historically restricts access to investigative files, citing ongoing investigations, witness protection concerns, and national security considerations.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

## Epstein Case Background

Jeffrey Epstein was convicted in 2008 on state charges of soliciting a minor in Florida and served 13 months in a work-release program.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> He was arrested again in July 2019 on federal charges of sex trafficking minors in Florida and New York.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

Epstein died in federal custody in August 2019 while awaiting trial on the federal sex trafficking charges.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> His associate Ghislaine Maxwell was convicted in December 2021 on federal sex trafficking charges and is currently serving a 20-year prison sentence.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Independent reported the breach Wednesday.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The outlet cited sources familiar with the incident but did not name them.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup> The report did not address whether the breach has impacted the FBI's ability to comply with the Transparency Act's disclosure requirements.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>

The Independent did not indicate whether lawmakers have been briefed on the breach or whether the FBI has implemented security measures in response to the intrusion.<sup>[1](#ref-1)</sup>
