At roughly 1:30 a.m. on Sunday, February 22, 2026, Secret Service agents shot and killed a man who had breached the secure perimeter of President Trump's Mar-a-Lago estate in Palm Beach, Florida. The man was carrying a shotgun and a gas can; when confronted, he set down the gas can but raised the weapon. Trump and his family were in Washington at the time.

## The Suspect

Authorities identified him as Austin Tucker Martin, 21, of Moore County, North Carolina. A self-described Trump supporter, Martin had — according to co-workers and investigators — grown increasingly fixated on the Epstein files after the latest document releases, convinced he was witnessing a government cover-up. Days earlier, on February 15, he texted a co-worker: "I don't know if you read up on the Epstein Files, but evil is real and unmistakable." Investigators reported finding Epstein-related materials in his car.

## Why It Belongs in the Record

The shooting was not part of the legal or legislative arc of the case, and Martin acted alone. But it marked something the document fight had produced in the country at large: a corrosive, conspiratorial intensity around the unreleased and over-redacted files. The FBI investigated his motive. The episode became a grim measure of how a transparency failure — files promised, then heavily withheld — curdled into radicalization for some who had believed the promises, and a reminder of the real-world stakes riding on how the government handled the disclosures.
